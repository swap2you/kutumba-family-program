# Executive Summary

KUTUMBA is a three-year family-formation pathway rooted primarily in Śrīla Prabhupāda’s teachings. It develops knowledge, skill, practice and character together. Adults and children study the same central truths at different developmental levels, practise them at home, serve within the temple and community, and build the capacity for sustained scriptural study.

The architecture is deliberately not a compressed Bhakti Śāstrī course. Year 1 establishes sambandha and family foundations. Year 2 develops abhidheya through a complete thematic survey of Bhagavad-gītā and regulated practice. Year 3 builds śāstric readiness, service ethics and bounded leadership. Formal degrees remain with authorised śāstric centres; initiation remains under the proper guru, temple and ISKCON process.

**CORE DESIGN DECISION  **Three years contain 156 calendar weeks but only 120 active KUTUMBA program weeks. Detailed curriculum production will therefore create 120 active-week modules, not 156 weekly lessons.

## Non-Negotiable Guardrails

- Śrīla Prabhupāda’s books control philosophical conclusions; supplementary material is reviewed and subordinate.

- KUTUMBA does not confer initiation, select an initiating guru, guarantee temple recommendations or replace the ISKCON Disciple Course.

- KUTUMBA does not issue a Bhakti Śāstrī degree or claim equivalent completion. Formal degrees are issued by authorised centres.

- Exact worship procedures, mantras and Deity-worship standards require qualified ISKCON review. No independent ritual innovation is permitted.

- Children’s safety overrides convenience, reputation, confidentiality and informal family culture.

- No public comparison of rounds, memorisation, cooking, singing, children, service or family practice.

- Temple participation increases rather than decreases. Festivals, pilgrimages and service replace a normal program week instead of adding chronic overload.

- Advanced or confidential topics are not used prematurely to create excitement, status or emotional effect.

**PART I**

**Curriculum Foundations**

Purpose, assumptions, formation model, source control and annual operating rhythm

# 1. Purpose and Destination

The curriculum exists to cultivate families whose homes, relationships, habits and service are increasingly centred on Kṛṣṇa. Its destination is not the accumulation of devotional information. Its destination is a family able to hear, chant, remember, worship, serve, associate, celebrate and make responsible spiritual decisions with increasing stability.

| Formation destination | What it means in practice |
| --- | --- |
| Stable devotional life | Regular, realistic practices that survive work pressure, travel, illness and imperfect weeks. |
| Stronger home sādhana | Chanting, hearing, food offering, prayer, cleanliness and service integrated into family life. |
| Temple connection | Participation in temple festivals, service, guidance and authorised developmental pathways. |
| Śāstric readiness | Ability to read, understand, discuss and apply Śrīla Prabhupāda’s books without speculation. |
| Service maturity | Reliability, cooperation, humility, safeguarding awareness and accountable use of authority. |
| Responsible transition | Clear distinction between KUTUMBA completion, Bhakti Śāstrī enrolment, Disciple Course and initiation processes. |

# 2. Key Assumptions

1. The founding cohort is approximately five to six families, with mixed ages and differing levels of devotional experience.

1. The program operates forty active weeks per year: thirty-six cycle weeks and four festival, service or pilgrimage programs.

1. Each learning cycle has six active weeks followed by one complete off week; additional holidays and buffers protect family capacity.

1. Adults and children share the same central theme, separate for age-appropriate formation, and reunite for practical bhakti and a family commitment.

1. The first six months are produced in full before launch; the remaining architecture is approved so future modules are generated without speculation.

1. Legal, child-protection, worship and accredited-course standards are verified at the point of use because those standards can change.

**REALITY CHECK  **A curriculum this broad fails if each session tries to teach every related doctrine. Detailed modules must select one principal conclusion, one practice and one measurable family application.

# 3. Four-Dimensional Formation Model

Figure 2. Every major topic produces knowledge, skill, practice and character evidence.

| Dimension | Curriculum question | Representative evidence | Failure mode if omitted |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Knowledge | What should the participant understand? | Explanation, story retelling, concept map, source identification. | Practice becomes sentimental or imitative. |
| Skill | What should the participant be able to do? | Demonstration, passage study, prayer, service, facilitation. | Knowledge remains theoretical. |
| Practice | What should become repeatable? | Private home-practice record, family routine, service continuity. | Inspiration disappears between meetings. |
| Character | What kind of person and family should emerge? | Speech, responsibility, cleanliness, humility, cooperation, safety. | Competence produces pride or harm. |

# 4. Sequence Logic and Prerequisites

The sequence is not arbitrary. Each stage resolves a problem required by the next stage:

**IDENTITY  →  CONDITIONED STATE  →  KṚṢṆA  →  AUTHORISED GUIDANCE  →  BHAKTI  →  HOME PRACTICE  →  REGULATION  →  STUDY  →  SERVICE  →  LEADERSHIP**

| Dependency rule | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| Karma follows body–soul identity. | Otherwise karma becomes material reward/punishment or victim-blame. |
| Guru-tattva follows Kṛṣṇa and guru–sādhu–śāstra. | Otherwise authority becomes personality loyalty. |
| Offenses follow a positive understanding of nāma and relationship. | Otherwise beginners receive fear before affection and purpose. |
| Worship procedure follows cleanliness, authority and service mood. | Otherwise ritual competence outruns responsibility. |
| Leadership follows repeated service and feedback. | Otherwise charisma is mistaken for qualification. |
| Advanced topics remain outside the general pathway. | Philosophical foundation, purification and qualified guidance cannot be bypassed. |
| Bhakti Śāstrī referral follows capacity and readiness review. | Attendance alone does not establish academic, behavioural or practical readiness. |

## 9. D1 — Spiritual Identity and Human Purpose

Body and soul; the eternal jīva; human responsibility; birth, death, old age and disease; freedom and dependence; temporary and permanent happiness; the constitutional relationship with Kṛṣṇa.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Distinguish body from self and state the purpose of human life. | Apply spiritual identity to duty, loss, success, family roles and decision-making. | Explain jīva-tattva coherently from multiple primary passages without reducing it to slogans. |

**BOUNDARY  **No speculative claims about a participant’s eternal identity or confidential spiritual form.

## 10. D2 — Bhagavat-tattva and Kṛṣṇa’s Supremacy

Kṛṣṇa as the Supreme Personality of Godhead; His names, qualities, forms, activities, associates and energies; Bhagavān, Paramātmā and Brahman; avatāras; Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Know Kṛṣṇa as the supreme person, source and loving protector. | Understand His material and spiritual energies, opulences, reciprocation and descent. | Present the personal conclusion of scripture while treating other conceptions accurately and respectfully. |

**BOUNDARY  **No independent rasa instruction or imaginative reconstruction of confidential pastimes.

## 11. D3 — Material Nature and the Conditioned State

Prakṛti, kāla, karma, māyā, false ego, mind, senses, three modes, attachment and broad creation principles.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Recognise action and reaction, reincarnation, the modes and māyā in simple life situations. | Examine intention, habit, association, consumption and duty through the modes. | Explain the conditioned state and broad creation principles without fatalism, blame or speculative cosmology. |

**BOUNDARY  **Do not turn Purāṇic cosmology into an amateur science debate or present uncertain harmonisations as siddhānta.

## 12. D4 — Guru, Sādhu, Śāstra and Paramparā

The need for received knowledge; functions and qualifications of guru; śikṣā and dīkṣā at an introductory level; disciplic succession; Śrīla Prabhupāda as ISKCON Founder-Ācārya; inquiry, service, discernment and accountability.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Know why authentic guidance is needed and how guru, sādhu and śāstra work together. | Practise respectful inquiry, service and discernment without personality dependence. | Explain guru-tattva, paramparā and initiation boundaries and know when to refer questions. |

**BOUNDARY  **KUTUMBA may not select a guru for anyone, promise initiation or compel timing or allegiance.

## 13. D5 — Śāstric Literacy and Study Method

Speaker, audience, context, translation, purport, structure, evidence, comparison, note-taking, summary, application and distinguishing scripture from opinion.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Read short passages and identify the main point. | Follow the argument and chapter movement of Bhagavad-gītā and use purports in discussion. | Construct source-grounded explanations, written responses and facilitation without speculation. |

**BOUNDARY  **Quotations may not be detached from context merely to support a preferred conclusion.

## 14. D6 — Nāma, Japa and Personal Sādhana

Hare Kṛṣṇa mahā-mantra, Pañca-tattva mantra, attentive chanting, prayer, dependence, ten offenses, hearing, remembrance and realistic household routines.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Understand the purpose of chanting and begin or stabilise an honest commitment. | Improve routine, attention and response to obstacles; understand offenses positively and practically. | Develop a sustainable plan, explain nāma-tattva carefully and support beginners without policing them. |

**BOUNDARY  **Round counts are private; no public comparison, spiritual ranking or pressure to make false commitments.

## 15. D7 — Nine Processes and Practical Bhakti

Śravaṇam, kīrtanam, smaraṇam, pāda-sevanam, arcanam, vandanam, dāsyam, sakhyam and ātma-nivedanam as a comprehensive devotional life.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Name the nine processes and experience several through family practice. | Select compatible practices and understand how they reinforce one another. | Facilitate practical bhakti laboratories and explain unity amid different services. |

**BOUNDARY  **No process is used as a status contest, and personal inclinations do not override authorised practice.

## 16. D8 — Home Devotional Culture and Worship

Sacred space, altar, food offering, prasāda, cleanliness, family reading, prayer, ārati awareness, Tulasī, tilaka, kanthi, Ekādaśī and home calendar.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Establish a simple, clean and workable home devotional rhythm. | Maintain it consistently and involve children in suitable responsibilities. | Demonstrate approved basics and help another family without inventing standards. |

**BOUNDARY  **Exact worship procedures and mantras require qualified ISKCON review; KUTUMBA does not author independent arcana standards.

## 17. D9 — Vaiṣṇava Character, Etiquette and Offenses

Humility, truthfulness, compassion, cleanliness, punctuality, gratitude, responsibility, speech, cooperation, forgiveness, reconciliation, Vaiṣṇava-aparādha and dealings with seniors, peers, juniors and guests.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Learn basic etiquette and recognise gossip, ridicule and disrespect. | Practise apology, reconciliation, boundaries and the six loving exchanges. | Model accountable conduct and address problems without faction, retaliation or concealment. |

**BOUNDARY  **Etiquette is not used to silence legitimate safety reports, questions or correction of abuse.

## 18. D10 — Family Formation and Responsible Household Life

Parents as examples; cooperation; devotional parenting; correction without humiliation; work, money and time responsibilities; screen culture; marriage and family boundaries; care without amateur therapy.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Build shared practices and visible parental participation. | Improve routines, communication, correction and cooperation under real pressures. | Form a continuing family plan and mentor within clear educational boundaries. |

**BOUNDARY  **KUTUMBA is not marriage counselling, psychotherapy, medical treatment or a forum for forced disclosure.

## 19. D11 — Sevā, Temple Relationship and Community Life

Service mood, reliability, capacity, authority, teamwork, hosting, cooking, cleanup, kīrtana support, festival preparation, devotee care, cow service and suitable outreach.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Take one trained, age-appropriate recurring service. | Complete service reliably and understand the relationship between KUTUMBA and temple life. | Coordinate a bounded service project and hand it over responsibly. |

**BOUNDARY  **KUTUMBA may not become a competing temple, faction or independent preaching centre.

## 20. D12 — Festivals, Dhāma and Pilgrimage

Theological meaning of festivals, appearance days, preparation, sacred places, proper dhāma conduct, pilgrimage as hearing and service, outdoor devotional activities and reflection.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Recognise principal festivals and connect them with Kṛṣṇa’s pastimes and devotees. | Prepare for and participate in festivals and pilgrimage with service and restraint. | Help facilitate reflection and protect pilgrimage from tourism, prestige and overload. |

**BOUNDARY  **Outings remain spiritually centred and replace, rather than add to, the normal weekly program.

## 21. D13 — Communication, Care and Servant Leadership

Listening, questions, facilitation, accountability, confidential care, boundaries, delegation, feedback, conflict handling, teaching discipline and admitting uncertainty.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Participate respectfully and fulfil simple commitments. | Use basic communication and reconciliation skills. | Facilitate a short session, coordinate service and refer care needs beyond competence. |

**BOUNDARY  **Leadership is never inferred from charisma, seniority, donations or knowledge alone.

## 22. D14 — Safeguarding and Ethical Boundaries

Two-adult and visible-space practice, safe communication, incident reporting, child and vulnerable-person protection, transportation and media consent, confidentiality limits and duty to report.

| Year 1 foundation | Year 2 development | Year 3 readiness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Know basic family and child safety expectations. | Follow approved procedures during programs and outings. | Identify risks, stop unsafe activity and escalate concerns through the authorised process. |

**BOUNDARY  **Spiritual language, confidentiality or reconciliation may never be used to conceal abuse or prevent mandatory or ethical reporting.

# 23. Year-Level Progression

## Year 1 — Sambandha and Family Foundations

**CONTROLLING QUESTION  **How do we practise bhakti consistently in real family life?

| Purpose | Entry expectation | Exit profile | Principal risks |

## Year 3 — Śāstric Readiness, Service and Leadership

**CONTROLLING QUESTION  **How can we become qualified students and responsible servants?

| Purpose | Entry expectation | Exit profile | Principal risks |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Build source discipline, study capacity, service ethics, safeguarding awareness and supervised facilitation while preserving clear boundaries around formal study and initiation. | Reasonable comprehension of the Year 2 Bhagavad-gītā map, reliable basic service and willingness to receive correction. | A participant can study, explain, facilitate and serve without speculation; a family has a continuing development plan; suitable adults can explore authorised Bhakti Śāstrī study. | Status seeking, pseudo-certification, premature teaching, guru politics, knowledge without character and leadership without accountability. |

# 52. Parallel Formation Model

Parents and children study the same central truth but not through the same method. The shared theme creates home conversation; developmental adaptation prevents children from becoming miniature adults or adults from receiving a children’s moral lesson.

| Practice | Personal and household implementation. | Safe age-appropriate participation. | Bhakti laboratory. |

# 53. Age-Band Strategy

Official ISKCON Sunday School material publishes age divisions of 4–5, 6–8, 9–11 and 12–14 and recommends adapting divisions when staffing or space is limited. KUTUMBA adopts those developmental anchors and adds a 15–17 youth band. In a small cohort, adjacent bands may combine, but each lesson must still provide layered tasks.

| Band | Age | Developmental priority | Preferred methods | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Early childhood | 4–5 | Relationship, imitation, rhythm, wonder and short routines. | Stories, movement, songs, sensory objects, one-step service and repetition. | No abstract testing; avoid fear-based karma/death content and long sitting. |
| Primary | 6–8 | Concrete sequence, belonging, rules and visible cause/effect. | Stories, crafts, role play, matching, call-response, simple verse meaning and two-step service. | Do not confuse memorisation with understanding. |
| Middle | 9–11 | Explanation, fairness, growing independence and collaborative projects. | Text finding, experiments, games, drama, short reflection and recurring service. | Avoid public comparison and oversimplified moral labels. |

**SMALL-COHORT RULE  **Combine adjacent ages by activity complexity, not by pretending one lesson suits everyone. Use a common story or principle, then provide tiered questions, service roles and evidence.

# 57. Assessment Without Competition

Assessment serves diagnosis and support. It asks what the learner understands, can perform, attempts consistently and demonstrates in conduct. It does not answer who is spiritually superior.

| Evidence type | Adults | Children / youth |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Explain | Short answer, concept map, verse meaning, chapter outline. | Story retelling, matching, drawing, oral explanation. |
| Demonstrate | Passage study, offering workflow, prayer, facilitation or service. | Prayer, verse, safe worship task, instrument care or service. |
| Apply | Family case, decision, schedule or repair plan. | Role play, scenario choice, project or service response. |
| Reflect | Private saṅkalpa, obstacle review and next step. | What I tried, what helped and what I will do next. |

| Private descriptor | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Introduced | Has encountered the teaching or skill and can participate with substantial support. |
| Developing | Understands or performs parts with prompts and correction. |
| Independent | Explains or performs the defined outcome without direct help. |
| Stable / can support | Applies with reasonable consistency and can assist another person within boundaries. |

- No ranking, published scores, spiritual titles, elimination quizzes or “best family” awards.

- No rewards for highest round count, most memorised verses, expensive prasāda or most visible service.

- A child may show learning through story, art, drama, project or service rather than recitation alone.

- Low performance triggers support, adaptation or a different pace—not humiliation.

- Attendance is evidence of exposure, not proof of understanding, practice or character.

**PART VI**

**Readiness, Transition and Completion**

Bhakti Śāstrī alignment, initiation boundaries and the three-year completion profile

# 60. Bhakti Śāstrī Readiness Boundary

KUTUMBA may determine that an adult appears ready to explore an authorised Bhakti Śāstrī program. It may not issue the degree, guarantee admission or present its own lessons as equivalent. The Ministry of Education maintains a list of centres authorised to issue śāstric degrees.

**CURRENT PUBLISHED STANDARDS  **The Ministry of Education currently lists minimum passing levels of 60% for open-book assessment, 65% for closed-book assessment and śloka assessment, adequate personal behaviour and 75% attendance per unit. It also publishes an optional 45-verse set. Centres retain defined discretion, so the chosen centre must be checked directly.

| KUTUMBA may say | KUTUMBA must not say |
| --- | --- |
| “This participant has demonstrated readiness-level competencies and should contact an authorised centre.” | “This participant has completed Bhakti Śāstrī.” |
| “The participant is familiar with the current optional 45-verse set.” | “The participant has satisfied every centre’s memorisation requirement.” |
| “The family has developed a meaningful devotional routine.” | “The participant is qualified for initiation.” |
| “The participant completed supervised facilitation and service.” | “The participant is entitled to lead or teach.” |

# 61. Readiness Criteria

| Area | Readiness indicator | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Academic comprehension | Can explain the purpose and structure of the four Bhakti Śāstrī books and outline the eighteen Bhagavad-gītā chapters. | Source-based explanation, chapter map and short written response. |
| Study method | Can identify context, use purports, cite references, answer the question asked and distinguish source from personal application. | Observed study task and open-book response. |
| Discussion and writing | Can listen, contribute concisely, write a coherent answer and accept correction. | Facilitated discussion and reviewed sample. |
| Memorisation capacity | Has substantial familiarity with the current 45-verse optional set and a realistic plan for the centre’s actual requirement. | Private oral sampling; no public competition. |
| Practice stability | Maintains meaningful chanting, hearing and service appropriate to current commitment and understands course expectations. | Private self-review and mentor conversation; no guarantee of admission. |
| Behaviour and cooperation | Generally demonstrates honesty, punctuality, respectful speech, teamwork and accountability. | Pattern across service and group participation, not a single incident. |
| Life capacity | Can protect class, reading, assessment and family time without foreseeable collapse. | Workload and family-impact review. |
| Authority transition | Has identified an authorised centre and will obtain its current admission, attendance and assessment requirements. | Direct confirmation with the chosen centre. |

**FAST VERIFICATION PLAN  **Before referral: open the Ministry’s current assessment and authorised-centre pages; contact the selected centre; obtain its admission, attendance, text, verse and assessment requirements; then update the private transition plan.

# 62. Initiation and Disciple-Course Transition

KUTUMBA prepares habits, knowledge, character and service culture. It does not conduct an initiation track. The GBC recommends that prospective disciples and aspiring initiates attend the ISKCON Disciple Course before initiation, preferably before choosing an initiating spiritual master. The GBC statement on Śrīla Prabhupāda’s preeminent position is also directed for study before initiation.

| Stage | KUTUMBA role | Proper external authority |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Interest in deeper commitment | Explain seriousness, encourage steady practice and remove social pressure. | Temple and authorised spiritual guides. |
| Questions about guru | Teach general guru–sādhu–śāstra and Founder-Ācārya principles; refer personal choice. | Authorised ISKCON process and prospective disciple’s responsible discernment. |
| Disciple Course | Provide orientation and help locate an authorised course. | Authorised ISKCON Disciple Course instructor/program. |
| Initiation application | No promise, certification or lobbying. | Relevant guru, temple authorities and current ISKCON procedures. |
| After initiation | Continue family formation and service without creating a separate loyalty structure. | Guru, temple and ISKCON authority relationships. |

**NON-COERCION  **No family is pressured to select a particular guru, pursue initiation on a timetable, or display aspiration publicly. Readiness is more important than social momentum.

# 68. Lesson Approval Gate

1. Source accuracy: quotations, references, conclusions and context verified.

1. Architecture fit: week serves the assigned cycle, domain and prerequisite without duplication.

1. Philosophical review: no speculation, contradiction, confidential-topic leakage or misleading simplification.

1. Age review: language, story, activity, cognitive load and behavioural expectations suit each band.

1. Worship review: any exact prayer, offering, altar or ārati procedure is reviewed by the qualified worship authority.

1. Safety review: child, travel, food, media, physical activity and emotional risks are controlled.

1. Practice realism: family commitment is bounded, measurable and survivable.

1. Assessment quality: evidence matches the outcome and does not create competition.

1. Copyright and repository review: permissions, links, metadata and versioning are correct.

1. Pilot and correction: facilitator rehearsal or pilot feedback is incorporated before approval.

**RELEASE RULE  **A teacher may use draft material only in a controlled pilot. Family-facing distribution requires the approved version stored in the source-of-truth repository.

# 71. KUTUMBA Setu — Late-Joiner Bridge

The main cohort does not restart when a family joins later. Admission is preferred at a cycle boundary. A mid-cycle family may observe up to two sessions and then completes the bridge before full participation.

| Setu component | Content | Completion evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Orientation | Vision, covenant, weekly flow, attendance, prasāda, safeguarding and communication. | Acknowledgement, forms and questions resolved. |
| 2. Foundational philosophy | Body/soul, Kṛṣṇa, karma, chanting and guru–sādhu–śāstra. | Concise conversation or guided digital module. |
| 3. Devotional culture | Kīrtana, temple/ārati etiquette, prasāda prayer, respectful speech and service. | Observed participation and etiquette check. |
| 4. Home practice | Realistic chanting, family reading, offering food, sacred space and one service. | Family plan and mentor confirmation. |

- Each new family receives a mentor family, concise digital welcome pack, core prayer sheet, glossary, child-placement conversation and allergy/safety forms.

- Previous experience is respected but mapped to KUTUMBA outcomes rather than accepted through title or self-description alone.

- At the next cycle boundary, the mentor confirms understanding, covenant willingness, realistic practice and absence of unresolved safety concerns.

- New families are welcomed warmly; the core curriculum is not diluted or repeatedly restarted.

# 72. Version and Change Control

| Change type | Approval | Version effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Typographical or formatting correction | Documentation owner | Patch version. |
| Example, timing or activity improvement with same outcome | Curriculum lead | Minor version. |
| Source, conclusion, outcome, sequence or boundary change | Curriculum/philosophy lead + affected authority | Major version or formally approved revision. |
| Safety, legal, worship or institutional-standard update | Responsible authority + program director | Immediate controlled update and affected-module review. |
| Pilot defect | Assigned owner + reviewer | Logged correction before general release. |

# 73. Implementation Sequence

1. Approve this architecture, including restricted topics and source hierarchy.

1. Complete governance, family covenant, safeguarding, prasāda, worship and service-roster prerequisites.

1. Produce the first three Year 1 cycles in full, including adult/child materials, labs, worksheets and teacher guides.

1. Review all exact prayers and worship procedures with qualified ISKCON guidance.

1. Orient founding families and complete Setu-equivalent baseline assessments.

1. Run the first cycle as a controlled pilot and correct operational defects.

1. Remain at least one fully approved cycle ahead; do not create all 120 lessons before learning from the pilot.

1. Review architecture annually, while protecting it from casual week-to-week redesign.

**RECOMMENDATION  **Lock the architecture now, but do not mass-produce 120 detailed modules before the first three cycles have been piloted. That would create rework and false precision. Build six months fully, pilot, then remain one cycle ahead under this architecture.
