## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| A family hears three different answers online to the same spiritual question. One speaker is charismatic, another has many followers and a third quotes a verse without context. The family asks, ‘How do we know what to accept?’ Devotional life requires faith, but not gullibility. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Source check: participants rank a viral quote, anonymous post, Śrīla Prabhupāda book, trained teacher and personal intuition by authority for doctrine.

1. 5–13 min — Read BG 4.34 and identify humility, inquiry, service and qualified knowledge.

1. 13–21 min — Guru–sādhu–śāstra triangle. Explain harmony and basic role distinctions.

1. 21–29 min — Case studies: charismatic contradiction, teacher speculation, pressure for initiation and a genuine safeguarding concern.

1. 29–35 min — Question clinic: convert vague, combative or dependent questions into specific, text-grounded inquiry.

1. 35–40 min — Choose one question, one source and one qualified person to approach.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Three helpers picture: sacred book, trustworthy devotee and teacher who follows the book.

- Follow-the-map game with visible route and adult guide.

- Practice asking: ‘Can you show me where this comes from?’

- Safety truth: tell parents or designated adults if any adult asks for secrecy or makes you unsafe.

### Age 7-9

- Guru–sādhu–śāstra triangle puzzle.

- Source sorting: book quote, teacher explanation, made-up claim and internet rumor.

- Write one respectful spiritual question.

- Learn that respect never means hiding unsafe behavior.

### Age 10-13

- Read BG 4.34 and discuss difference between inquiry and argument for ego.

- Analyze authority claims: title, popularity, behavior, source consistency and accountability.

- Practice verifying a quote in an assigned primary source.

- Review digital and child-safety boundaries with spiritual teachers and mentors.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- What three approaches are named in BG 4.34?

- What should characterize a spiritual master in ŚB 11.3.21?

- What does CC Madhya 20.122 say about the living entity’s constitutional position?

### Understanding

- Why should guru, sādhu and śāstra harmonize?

- Why is humility compatible with verification?

- What authority does KUTUMBA not possess?

### Application

- What question needs an authoritative answer?

- Which primary source can you consult first?

- Who is qualified to answer or refer it?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Source Verification and Question Practice

1. Provide a short claim and three possible sources.

1. Participants locate the verse or approved excerpt in a printed/digital index.

1. Write the claim, source, context and practical conclusion.

1. Role-play asking a qualified teacher using humility, specific inquiry and willingness to serve.

1. If the answer is uncertain, practice saying: ‘I do not know; I will verify and return.’

## Teacher Preparation

- Read BG 4.34, SB 11.3.21, CC Madhya 20.122 and KUTUMBA authority policies.

- Confirm exact language with the temple liaison for initiation, guru and worship boundaries.

- Prepare source-verification exercises using only authorized texts.

- Review child-protection escalation and explain that misconduct reports are not gossip or offense when handled properly.

- Instruct teachers to say ‘I do not know’ rather than improvise.
