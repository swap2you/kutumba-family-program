# C3-W1 — Who Is God? The Supreme Enjoyer, Proprietor and Friend

*Cycle 3 — Kṛṣṇa, Guru and the Process of Bhakti*

| **Field** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- |
| **Week code** | C3-W1 |
| **Cycle function** | Foundational principle |
| **Purpose** | Give families a clear personal conception of God as the supreme conscious controller, proprietor, enjoyer, witness and well-wishing friend, and show how this knowledge brings practical peace. |
| **Key verse** | Bhagavad-gītā 5.29 — bhoktāraṁ yajña-tapasāṁ ... suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānām |
| **Memory line** | Peace comes from knowing Kṛṣṇa as the supreme enjoyer, proprietor of everything and friend of all living beings. |
| **Bhakti laboratory** | Stewardship Seva and Simple Offering Mood |

## Learning Outcomes

| **Outcome domain** | **By the end of the session, participants should be able to…** |
| --- | --- |
| **Knowledge** | State the three peace-giving features in BG 5.29: supreme enjoyer, proprietor and friend of all beings. |
| **Skill** | Reframe one conflict over control, credit or possession using the peace formula. |
| **Practice** | Use one daily ‘Kṛṣṇa owns, Kṛṣṇa enjoys, Kṛṣṇa is friend’ remembrance before a family duty. |

## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| Two children argue over a room full of toys: ‘Mine!’ ‘No, mine!’ A parent intervenes, but later the adults have the same argument in a more sophisticated form—over money, credit, schedule, recognition or authority. Much family conflict begins with a hidden claim: ‘I am the owner, I am the enjoyer, and my plan should control everyone.’ |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Possessive-language audit: list common ‘my’ claims in a family without public disclosure.

1. 5–13 min — Read BG 5.29 and identify enjoyer, proprietor, friend and the result—peace.

1. 13–20 min — Compare individual soul, material nature, Supersoul and Supreme Person using a simple four-part diagram.

1. 20–29 min — Case studies: money decision, child achievement, service credit and household authority. Apply the three peace questions.

1. 29–35 min — Stewardship versus neglect: discuss time, body, property and relationships as entrusted resources.

1. 35–40 min — Choose one daily duty and prepare a three-line remembrance before it.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Shared basket activity: materials belong to Kṛṣṇa and are cared for by everyone.

- Three-picture chant: Kṛṣṇa receives, Kṛṣṇa owns, Kṛṣṇa is friend.

- Practice offering a flower or drawing with love, without teaching unapproved ritual details.

- Stewardship job: return one item clean and complete.

### Age 7-9

- Peace-formula triangle labeled Enjoyer, Proprietor and Friend.

- Scenario sort: grabbing, wasting, sharing, caring and offering.

- Discuss why ‘Kṛṣṇa owns it’ means greater care, not careless use.

- Write one gratitude note for an entrusted resource.

### Age 10-13

- Read BG 5.29 and explain why each feature matters for peace.

- Analyze creator, owner and user claims in digital content or group projects.

- Discuss the difference between God as person and God as only energy.

- Apply stewardship to money, talent, time or environmental care.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- What three identities of Kṛṣṇa appear in BG 5.29?

- What result comes from knowing them?

- What does BG 9.10 indicate about material nature?

### Understanding

- Why does Kṛṣṇa’s ownership create stewardship rather than neglect?

- How does seeking Kṛṣṇa’s satisfaction reduce competition?

- Why is a well-wishing friend qualified to guide?

### Application

- What are you treating as exclusively ‘mine’?

- Whose approval are you seeking in a current duty?

- What decision better serves all souls involved?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Stewardship Seva and Simple Offering Mood

1. Choose a shared resource: books, instruments, altar cloths or classroom supplies.

1. Inspect, clean, count and return the items according to an approved checklist.

1. Before beginning, state: ‘These resources belong to Kṛṣṇa; we care for them for service.’

1. Demonstrate the mood of offering a flower, water or drawing with love, while noting that exact worship procedures require approved guidance.

1. Close with gratitude for the resource and the people who maintain it.

## Teacher Preparation

- Read BG 5.29, 9.10, 13.23, 9.26 and Īśopaniṣad Mantra 1 with purports.

- Prepare the four-part diagram and avoid overcomplicated tattva terminology.

- Select shared materials for a real stewardship task.

- Brief teachers not to use God’s ownership to pressure donations or control personal property.

- Prepare interfaith-sensitive wording: teach KUTUMBA’s siddhānta without mocking other conceptions.
