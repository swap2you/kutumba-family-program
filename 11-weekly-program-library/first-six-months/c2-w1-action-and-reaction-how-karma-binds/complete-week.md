# C2-W1 — Action and Reaction: How Karma Binds

*Cycle 2 — Karma, Rebirth and Material Nature*

| **Field** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- |
| **Week code** | C2-W1 |
| **Cycle function** | Foundational principle |
| **Purpose** | Introduce karma as the law by which materially motivated actions produce consequences and bind the living being, while showing that work offered for Viṣṇu can be purified. |
| **Key verse** | Bhagavad-gītā 3.9 — yajñārthāt karmaṇo ’nyatra ... karma-bandhanaḥ |
| **Memory line** | Work done only for separate material interest causes bondage; work done for Viṣṇu should be performed without selfish attachment. |
| **Bhakti laboratory** | Before–During–After Service Method |
| **Minimum home practice** | Use the before–during–after method once on a routine duty. |

## Learning Outcomes

| **Outcome domain** | **By the end of the session, participants should be able to…** |
| --- | --- |
| **Knowledge** | Define karma in this lesson as action and reaction under material nature, not merely fate or punishment. |

| **Practice** | Transform one routine duty by performing it carefully and offering the result to Kṛṣṇa. |

## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| A parent sends a hurried message in anger. The message takes ten seconds to send, but the reaction continues for days. Trust is damaged, explanations multiply and the original issue becomes harder to solve. Another parent patiently repairs a child’s school project. The child feels supported, yet the parent secretly expects praise and becomes resentful when none comes. Actions are not isolated dots. They carry intention, method and consequence. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Retrieval bridge from Cycle 1: if the soul is eternal, why do actions in a temporary body still matter?

1. 5–12 min — Read BG 3.9. Define action, reaction, bondage and yajña-centered work in plain language.

1. 12–20 min — Three-lens analysis: intention, method and consequence. Apply to cooking, earning, correction and online speech.

1. 20–28 min — King Nṛga summary: good intention, procedural error, unresolved conflict and reaction. Avoid detailed cosmology.

1. 28–35 min — Case cards: truthful but harsh speech, generous gift for praise, careless service and difficult duty offered sincerely.

1. 35–40 min — Each adult writes one routine duty and a before–during–after service plan.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Plant two quick-growing seeds or use picture seeds; connect kind choices with helpful results.

- Action echo game: teacher names an action, children show a likely immediate result.

- Simple story: returning a borrowed item carefully versus losing it through carelessness.

- Recall: ‘Choices are seeds. I want to plant service seeds.’

### Age 7-9

- Seed–fruit matching cards, including delayed results such as practice leading to skill.

- Child-level King Nṛga summary emphasizing care and correction, not fear.

- Analyze intention–method–result in three school or sibling situations.

- Choose one duty to do as an offering without asking for praise.

### Age 10-13

- Read BG 3.9 and distinguish stopping work from changing its purpose.

- Discuss why good intention does not excuse dishonest or careless method.

- Challenge the statement: ‘Every suffering person deserves what happened.’

- Create an action chain showing direct, indirect and delayed effects of one online post.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- What kind of work does BG 3.9 say causes bondage?

- What elements besides the visible act shape its moral and devotional quality?

- What did King Nṛga’s situation show about care and accountability?

### Understanding

- Why is karma not identical to fatalism?

- Why can we not diagnose another person’s suffering confidently?

- How does offering work to Kṛṣṇa differ from merely saying ‘for Kṛṣṇa’ after careless action?

### Application

- Which routine action is most often driven by recognition, anger or haste?

- Who may be affected by that action?

- What specific change in intention or method will reduce bondage?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Before–During–After Service Method

1. Choose one simple task: setting the prasāda area, arranging shoes or preparing class materials.

1. Before: state purpose, safety and who is served.

1. During: work carefully, cooperate and notice the demand for praise.

1. After: inspect the result, correct defects and offer gratitude.

1. Families identify which stage they usually skip.

## Teacher Preparation

- Read BG 3.9, 4.16–18 and the King Nṛga chapter; prepare a restrained summary.

- Do not present a complete taxonomy of karma, vikarma and akarma beyond the level supported by the lesson.

- Prepare scenarios that involve ordinary family responsibility rather than extreme tragedy.

- Brief teachers to stop victim-blaming language immediately and calmly.

- Prepare child-safe seed activity and check allergies if soil is used.
