## Opening Hook (modern scenario)

_This is a contemporary entry point — not Prem-kī-Kathā. See [`prem-ki-katha.md`](prem-ki-katha.md) for the devotional narrative._

| A child asks at bedtime, 'What happens when someone dies?' Adults often move toward one of two extremes. We may avoid the question because it is uncomfortable, or give a frightening amount of detail because we think philosophy requires bluntness. Kṛṣṇa gives a sober middle path: the body changes and ends, but the soul is not destroyed. |
| --- |
