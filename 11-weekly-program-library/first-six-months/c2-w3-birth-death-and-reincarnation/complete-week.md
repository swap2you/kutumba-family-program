# C2-W3 — Birth, Death and Reincarnation

*Cycle 2 — Karma, Rebirth and Material Nature*

| **Field** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- |
| **Week code** | C2-W3 |
| **Cycle function** | Practical worldview laboratory |
| **Purpose** | Teach transmigration as the soul’s movement through changing bodies under karma and consciousness, in a calm and age-appropriate way that supports responsibility and spiritual hope rather than fear or speculation. |
| **Key verse** | Bhagavad-gītā 2.22 — vāsāṁsi jīrṇāni yathā vihāya |
| **Memory line** | As a person puts aside worn clothes and accepts new ones, the soul leaves an old body and receives another. |

## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| A child asks at bedtime, ‘What happens when someone dies?’ Adults often move toward one of two extremes. We may avoid the question because it is uncomfortable, or give a frightening amount of detail because we think philosophy requires bluntness. Kṛṣṇa gives a sober middle path: the body changes and ends, but the soul is not destroyed. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Anonymous question box: participants may submit a question about death or reincarnation without discussion of personal loss.

1. 5–13 min — Read BG 2.13 and 2.22. Identify continuity, change and analogy limits.

1. 13–20 min — Read the memory line from BG 8.6. Explain life-direction and repeated consciousness rather than superstition about one thought.

1. 20–30 min — Response practice: answer a five-year-old, ten-year-old and grieving adult in two or three sentences.

1. 30–35 min — Misconception check: ghost stories, choosing any next body, guessing a deceased person’s destination and minimizing grief.

1. 35–40 min — Design a one-minute bedtime remembrance practice.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Use a doll with changeable clothes: the doll/person stays while clothes change; explain simply that the soul stays while bodies change.

- Read a gentle original story about a caterpillar leaving one stage and continuing, while noting it is only a picture, not proof.

- Feelings cards: sad, confused, peaceful, worried. All can be shared with a trusted adult.

- Practice a bedtime prayer: ‘Kṛṣṇa, please help me remember You.’

### Age 7-9

- Sequence BG 2.13: baby, child, adult, old body, another body.

- Clothes analogy and its limit: bodies are not chosen from a closet.

- Question sorting: what scripture tells us, what we can infer and what we should not guess.

- Create a remembrance card for bedtime.

### Age 10-13

- Compare BG 2.13, 2.20 and 2.22 in a three-column chart.

- Discuss how repeated media, desires and habits shape consciousness.

- Analyze compassionate and uncompassionate responses to grief.

- Prepare a two-sentence answer to ‘Why should present choices matter if the soul continues?’

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- What changes are named before death in BG 2.13?

- What continues in BG 2.20?

- What comparison is used in BG 2.22?

### Understanding

- Why is the clothing analogy helpful and limited?

- How does repeated consciousness relate to future direction?

- Why should grief be respected even when the soul is eternal?

### Application

- What fills the final minutes of your day now?

- What simple remembrance can replace one minute of distraction?

- How will you answer a child without fear or false certainty?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Bedtime Remembrance Routine

1. Choose a fixed cue: device charging, teeth brushing or entering the bedroom.

1. Sit or stand safely before the sacred space or a clean image.

1. Chant the mahā-mantra once slowly or hear a short approved recording.

1. Name one gratitude and one request for remembrance.

1. End without extending into a lecture or forced emotional sharing.

## Teacher Preparation

- Read the assigned verses and prepare only the level needed for foundational teaching.

- Check whether any participating family has a recent bereavement; offer an opt-out or private warning.

- Prepare gentle materials; no graphic images, frightening stories or public testimony requests.

- Brief teachers not to interpret dreams, paranormal claims or a specific person’s rebirth.

- Have a plan to refer intense grief or trauma to appropriate pastoral or professional support.

## Assessment

- Adult explains reincarnation using continuity and change without speculation.

- Adult gives a compassionate response that allows grief.

- Child states that the soul continues and knows to ask a trusted adult.

- No public quiz about destinations, species or personal deceased relatives.
