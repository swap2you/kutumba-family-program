# C3-W2 — Who Is Kṛṣṇa? The Supreme Personality of Godhead

*Cycle 3 — Kṛṣṇa, Guru and the Process of Bhakti*

| **Field** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- |
| **Week code** | C3-W2 |
| **Cycle function** | Scriptural story and application |
| **Purpose** | Establish Kṛṣṇa’s unique supreme position from Śrīla Prabhupāda’s primary sources while introducing His attractive personal qualities and pastimes without premature or speculative treatment of confidential subjects. |
| **Key verse** | Bhagavad-gītā 7.7 — mattaḥ parataraṁ nānyat kiñcid asti dhanañjaya |

## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| A child asks, ‘There are so many powerful beings and so many names. Why does KUTUMBA say Kṛṣṇa is Supreme?’ The correct answer is not ‘because our group says so.’ Devotional conviction should be grounded in śāstra as presented by the ācārya. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Opening question: ‘What evidence would distinguish conviction from preference?’

1. 5–15 min — Text triangle: BG 7.7, BG 10.8 and ŚB 1.3.28. Groups summarize what each adds.

1. 15–22 min — Read a brief summary from the Kṛṣṇa Book Introduction on Kṛṣṇa’s supreme identity.

1. 22–30 min — Govardhana case: identify Kṛṣṇa’s supremacy, protection, reciprocation and correction of pride.

1. 30–35 min — Respectful explanation practice: answer a sincere question without attack or vague universalism.

1. 35–40 min — Select one authorized name/quality/pastime for daily remembrance.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Tell Govardhana-līlā using authorized, simple details and a felt-board or figures.

- Quality cards: strong, protective, playful, loving and all-attractive.

- Pearl necklace demonstration with visible thread.

- Recall: ‘Kṛṣṇa is the Supreme Lord and protects His devotees.’

### Age 7-9

- Sequence Govardhana events and identify what each event shows about Kṛṣṇa.

- Read the English memory line of BG 7.7 and build a pearl-thread craft.

- Sort claims into verse-supported, story-supported and invented.

- Choose one Kṛṣṇa name and write its remembered quality.

### Age 10-13

- Compare the claims of BG 7.7, BG 10.8 and ŚB 1.3.28.

- Discuss why extraordinary childhood pastimes do not make Kṛṣṇa an ordinary mythic hero.

- Analyze respectful conviction versus aggressive debate and vague relativism.

- Prepare a two-minute authorized pastime retelling with source, principle and application.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- What claim does Kṛṣṇa make in BG 7.7?

- What source relationship is stated in BG 10.8?

- How does ŚB 1.3.28 describe Kṛṣṇa among avatāras?

### Understanding

- Why is Kṛṣṇa’s supremacy personal and attractive rather than merely forceful?

- What does the thread analogy explain and not explain?

- How can conviction remain respectful?

### Application

- Which name or quality of Kṛṣṇa will you remember?

- Which authorized pastime can your family retell accurately?

- How will this understanding affect your chanting or offering?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Authorized Kṛṣṇa-Kathā Retelling

1. Use a four-card structure: source, event, quality of Kṛṣṇa, family application.

1. Facilitator models a three-minute Govardhana retelling without invented dialogue.

1. Families prepare a ninety-second retelling from supplied source summaries.

1. Listeners identify the quality and application, not performance style.

1. Teacher corrects factual errors kindly after the retelling.

## Teacher Preparation

- Read the assigned verses, Kṛṣṇa Book Introduction and selected Govardhana passages.

- Keep confidential Vraja topics outside this foundational module.

- Prepare visual aids that are respectful and not entertainment-heavy.

- Brief teachers to avoid interfaith debate and disparagement of devas or other traditions.
