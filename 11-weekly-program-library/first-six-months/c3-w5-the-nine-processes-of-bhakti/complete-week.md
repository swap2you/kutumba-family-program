# C3-W5 — The Nine Processes of Bhakti

*Cycle 3 — Kṛṣṇa, Guru and the Process of Bhakti*

| **Field** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- |
| **Week code** | C3-W5 |
| **Cycle function** | Case study, practice and service |
| **Purpose** | Give families a broad, scriptural map of devotional service so bhakti is experienced as a whole life of hearing, chanting, remembering, worship, prayer, service, friendship and surrender—not only classroom attendance. |
| **Key verse** | Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 7.5.23–24 — śravaṇaṁ kīrtanaṁ viṣṇoḥ smaraṇaṁ pāda-sevanam |
| **Memory line** | Hearing, chanting, remembering, serving, worshiping, praying, serving as a servant, friendship and full surrender are processes of pure devotional service. |
| **Bhakti laboratory** | Nine-Process Bhakti Stations |
| **Minimum home practice** | Continue the family’s core practice and add one underused process once. |

## Learning Outcomes

| **Outcome domain** | **By the end of the session, participants should be able to…** |
| --- | --- |
| **Knowledge** | Name the nine processes from ŚB 7.5.23–24 at recognition level. |
| **Skill** | Diagnose which devotional processes are present or absent in a family routine. |
| **Practice** | Strengthen one underused process while continuing the family’s core hearing and chanting. |
| **Character** | Service variety, appreciation of different strengths, steadiness and surrender. |
| **Child** | Recognize all nine processes through pictures and actions. |

## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| A family says, ‘We do bhakti every Friday.’ But during the week, no one hears, chants, remembers, prays, serves or offers anything. Another family chants daily but never serves together and becomes critical of others. A third enjoys festivals but avoids scripture. Each has something valuable, yet bhakti has become narrow. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Brain dump: what activities does your family currently call bhakti?

1. 5–15 min — Read ŚB 7.5.23–24 in sections and build a nine-process wall map.

1. 15–22 min — Clarify Viṣṇu/Kṛṣṇa-centered object and the difference between devotional form and generic virtue.

1. 22–30 min — Family routine audit: mark strong, occasional and absent processes without public scoring.

1. 30–35 min — Case: one-process pride, activity without hearing and study without service.

1. 35–40 min — Create a seven-day bhakti menu with one core practice and one underused process only.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Nine-process action circle with picture icons and simple gestures.

- Prahlāda story focus: he remembers and speaks about Kṛṣṇa.

- Choose a favorite process picture and perform one safe action.

- Make a nine-petal flower craft with adult help.

### Age 7-9

- Match nine processes with famous examples from the purport at recognition level.

- Bhakti charades using only respectful actions.

- Design a three-item family bhakti menu.

- Practice the first line of the verse by rhythm and gesture.

### Age 10-13

- Read ŚB 7.5.23–24 and categorize processes as receiving, expressing, serving and relating while noting overlap.

- Analyze why a process needs Kṛṣṇa-centered object and intention.

- Discuss one-process perfection without using it as an excuse to avoid other duties.

- Take responsibility for one trained service in the integration week.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- What are the nine processes named by Prahlāda?

- Which processes already appear in the Friday program?

- What does the purport say about one process practiced without deviation?

### Understanding

- Why is bhakti more than generic kindness or cultural activity?

- How can different processes strengthen one another?

- Why should different family strengths not become rankings?

### Application

- Which process is strongest in your family?

- Which important process is underused?

- What one small action will add it without overload?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Nine-Process Bhakti Stations

1. Set up brief stations: hear a verse, respond in kīrtana, remember a quality, clean a sacred space, observe approved worship items, write a prayer, complete a service, make a friendship promise to Kṛṣṇa and place a plan before Kṛṣṇa.

1. Exact worship actions are demonstration-only unless approved and trained.

1. Families rotate in small groups; each station lasts two minutes.

1. At the end, each person chooses one process that felt natural and one to learn.

## Teacher Preparation

- Read ŚB 7.5.23–24 and purport carefully; prepare the traditional example names only if teachers can present them accurately.

- Coordinate station content with the worship lead so no unapproved procedure is taught.

- Prepare icons and short age-appropriate actions for all nine processes.

- Avoid overloading families with nine home commitments.

- Brief teachers that personality preference is not the sole authority for selecting practice.
