# C3-W4 — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and the Holy Name

*Cycle 3 — Kṛṣṇa, Guru and the Process of Bhakti*

| **Field** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- |
| **Week code** | C3-W4 |
| **Cycle function** | Parent and child formation |
| **Purpose** | Introduce Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu’s gift of congregational chanting and help families experience attentive, humble and joyful nāma practice without performance pressure or claims of advanced realization. |
| **Key verse** | Caitanya-caritāmṛta Antya 20.12 — Śikṣāṣṭakam 1 — ceto-darpaṇa-mārjanaṁ ... paraṁ vijayate śrī-kṛṣṇa-saṅkīrtanam |
| **Memory line** | The congregational chanting of Kṛṣṇa’s holy name cleanses the heart and expands spiritual life. |
| **Bhakti laboratory** | Attentive Family Kīrtana |
| **Minimum home practice** | Five-minute family kīrtana or attentive mantra listening on three days. |

## Learning Outcomes

| **Outcome domain** | **By the end of the session, participants should be able to…** |
| --- | --- |
| **Knowledge** | Identify Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu as Kṛṣṇa who taught and spread saṅkīrtana according to Gauḍīya Vaiṣṇava understanding. |
| **Skill** | Participate in call-and-response kīrtana with attention to hearing and appropriate volume. |
| **Practice** | Complete one five-minute family kīrtana or japa-listening practice on three days. |
| **Character** | Humility, joy, perseverance and non-performance. |
| **Child** | Recognize Lord Caitanya and the Pañca-tattva and participate respectfully in the mahā-mantra. |

## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| A family begins kīrtana. One child sings loudly and proudly, another is shy, one adult worries about melody, and another silently checks whether people are impressed. The holy name is present, but the mind has turned chanting into performance. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Listening test: hear one softly spoken mantra and write which words attention actually caught.

1. 5–13 min — Read the memory line from Śikṣāṣṭakam 1. Explain mirror, fire and expanding ocean as teaching images.

1. 13–20 min — Short authorized account of Lord Caitanya’s saṅkīrtana mission and Jharikhaṇḍa, avoiding sensationalism.

1. 20–28 min — Kīrtana etiquette: leader responsibility, response, volume, tempo, instruments and inclusion.

1. 28–34 min — Attention practice: hear one mantra, notice wandering, return without self-attack.

1. 34–40 min — Design the family five-minute practice and select melody, place, cue and child role.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Mirror cleaning demonstration with a safely smudged acrylic mirror.

- Pañca-tattva picture introduction and call-and-response names.

- Animal movement kīrtana inspired by Jharikhaṇḍa, kept orderly and brief.

- Listening game: raise a hand when the full mahā-mantra ends.

### Age 7-9

- Sequence Lord Caitanya’s gift: chant, hear, cleanse, remember, share.

- Kīrtana role cards: leader, responder, kartāl support, respectful listener and space helper.

- Attention bead: move one bead after hearing one complete mantra.

- Discuss why skill and devotion are not the same measurement.

### Age 10-13

- Read the English meaning of Śikṣāṣṭakam 1 and interpret the three main images.

- Analyze performance motives in music and social media without judging individuals.

- Practice leading two moderate call-and-response mantras with a clear ending.

- Create an attention-recovery plan for japa or kīrtana.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- What effects of saṅkīrtana are named in Śikṣāṣṭakam 1?

- What did Lord Caitanya and His associates distribute?

- What is the first practical action when attention wanders?

### Understanding

- Why is the mirror image useful?

- Why is musical skill not proof of spiritual advancement?

- What is the difference between joyful expression and imitation of advanced symptoms?

### Application

- What cue will begin your five-minute family practice?

- How will every child have a safe role?

- What will you do when someone is distracted or reluctant?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Attentive Family Kīrtana

1. Review the Pañca-tattva mantra and mahā-mantra according to approved local practice.

1. Leader uses one simple melody and moderate tempo for two short rounds.

1. Responders listen before answering; kartāls, if used, follow the trained pattern and volume.

1. Pause for ten seconds and ask: which word did you hear most clearly?

1. Repeat with improved attention and a clean ending.

## Teacher Preparation

- Read CC Antya 20.12, CC Ādi 7.163, CC Ādi 17.21 and the selected Jharikhaṇḍa account.

- Confirm local approved mantra order, melodies, instrument patterns and worship etiquette with the kīrtana/worship lead.

- Prepare a simple melody within the leader’s competence.

- Brief teachers: no imitation of tears, shaking or advanced symptoms; no child performance ranking.

- Check instrument safety, volume and two-adult supervision during movement activities.
