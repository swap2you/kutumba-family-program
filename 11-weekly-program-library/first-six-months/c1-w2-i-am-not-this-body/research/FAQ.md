# C1-W2 FAQ

## Discovery

**What bodily stages does BG 2.13 name?**  
Childhood (kaumāra), youth (yauvana), and old age (jarā). See [verse study](VERSE-AND-REFERENCE-STUDY.md).

**Who passes through those stages?**  
The embodied soul (*dehī*), not the body as the self.

**What evidence do we already have?**  
Everyone recognizes "that was me" in old photos despite bodily change.

## Understanding

**Does "not the body" mean the body is bad?**  
No. It means the body is not the full self. Care for it as Kṛṣṇa's instrument.

**Can children understand this?**  
Yes — photo timeline and costume relay convey continuity without metaphysics.

**Why not teach death and next life this week?**  
Scope control. Garment verse hints at change; C1-W3 carries transmigration detail.

**How does this relate to BG 13?**  
Body = field; you = knower. Introduced briefly; full chapter later.

## Application

**What is the identity pause?**  
At a daily trigger: three breaths, recall "I am Kṛṣṇa's servant; this body is for service," optional short chant.

**What if someone struggles with body image?**  
Never shame. Use language rewrite. Encourage professional help if needed — philosophy supplements, not replaces, care.

**What if a newcomer finds Sanskrit overwhelming?**  
Use [newcomer-adaptation.md](../newcomer-adaptation.md) — English first, one verse summary.
