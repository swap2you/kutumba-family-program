# C1-W2 Verse and Reference Study

Original KUTUMBA summaries only. Read full text and purport on VedaBase.

---

## Bhagavad-gītā 2.13

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/13/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/13/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** Kṛṣṇa describes how the embodied soul passes through bodily changes — from boyhood to youth to old age — and will similarly pass into another body at death. A sober, wise person is not bewildered by such bodily change.

**Teaching use this week:** Primary anchor. Identify three named stages, the subject who passes through them, and the call to sobriety.

**Not this week:** Detailed description of the next body or dying process.

---

## Bhagavad-gītā 2.16

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/16/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/16/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** That which is temporary has no enduring reality; the eternal soul has no end. Those who see the truth of spirit and matter do not grieve over the body's change as if the self were destroyed.

**Teaching use:** Support distinction between changing body and enduring self; caution against treating body as "unreal" excuse for neglect.

---

## Bhagavad-gītā 2.20

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/20/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/20/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** The soul is never born, never dies, and is not destroyed when the body is destroyed. It is unborn, eternal, ever-existing, and primeval.

**Teaching use:** Youth extension on disability and dignity — injury changes the body, does not "erase" the person.

---

## Bhagavad-gītā 2.22

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/22/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/22/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** As a person gives up old and worn garments for new ones, the soul accepts new material bodies, giving up old and useless ones.

**Teaching use:** Garment/costume analogy in session. Full transmigration teaching deferred to C1-W3.

---

## Bhagavad-gītā 13.1

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/13/1/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/13/1/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** This body is called the *kṣetra* (field); one who knows this body is called the *kṣetrajña* (knower of the field).

**Teaching use:** Observer/field diagram for ages 9+; brief parent reference.

---

## Bhagavad-gītā 13.2

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/13/2/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/13/2/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** The living entity is the knower of the field; the field itself is material. Understanding this distinction is true knowledge.

---

## Bhagavad-gītā 13.3

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/13/3/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/13/3/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** Kṛṣṇa begins enumerating what the field comprises — elements, ego, senses, and related factors — showing the body as an object of analysis, not the self.

**Teaching use:** Teaser only; full chapter 13 belongs to later curriculum.

---

## Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 10.84.13

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/sb/10/84/13/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/sb/10/84/13/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** Persons who identify the self with the body, family, home, wealth, or children are compared to animals in human form — bodily identification blocks spiritual understanding.

**Teaching use:** Parent misconception check — "I am my job/status" is bodily/worldly identification.

---

## Caitanya-caritāmṛta Madhya 20.108

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/cc/madhya/20/108/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/cc/madhya/20/108/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** The living entity's constitutional position (*svarūpa*) is eternal service to Kṛṣṇa (*jīvera 'svarūpa' haya — kṛṣṇera 'nitya-dāsa'*).

**Teaching use:** Positive identity statement after negation — servant of Kṛṣṇa.

---

## Caitanya-caritāmṛta Madhya 20.109

**Link:** [https://vedabase.io/en/library/cc/madhya/20/109/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/cc/madhya/20/109/)

**KUTUMBA summary:** Lord Caitanya teaches that this servant relationship is the jīva's eternal nature — identity is defined by relationship to Kṛṣṇa, not bodily label.

**Teaching use:** Closing sankalpa and service-based identity for youth.

## Study sequence for facilitators

1. Read BG 2.13 on VedaBase (full verse + purport privately)
2. Read BG 2.22 for garment analogy
3. Skim BG 13.1–3 for field/knower language
4. Read CC Madhya 20.108–109 for positive identity
5. Use SB 10.84.13 for parent status/attachment discussion

Do not read all purports aloud in session.
