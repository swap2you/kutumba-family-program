## Newcomer Adaptation

Apply KUTUMBA Setu principles: reduce Sanskrit load, explain terms once, offer opt-in participation, and never pressure personal disclosure.

| **KUTUMBA Setu application** | **C1-W2 implementation** |
| --- | --- |
| **Terminology** | Introduce *deha* (body) and *dehī* (embodied self) once with plain English. Use "I have a body" before "I am not this body." |
| **Scripture access** | Read KUTUMBA summary of [BG 2.13](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/13/) aloud; offer optional lookup on VedaBase after session |
| **Chanting** | Identity pause may use silent reflection or listening to one brief kīrtana if japa feels unfamiliar |
| **Ritual posture** | Sitting comfortably is sufficient; no requirement to touch feet or advanced etiquette |
| **Philosophy depth** | Stay with one verse (2.13) and one analogy (photos or garments); defer BG 13 and CC until optional reading |
| **Child inclusion** | Newcomer children join Lāla–Lālī core track; parents may stay with child for costume relay |
| **Social comfort** | Case studies discussed in pairs, not forced public sharing of body-image struggles |
| **Belief spectrum** | Frame as "Vedic insight" and "observation exercise," not debate victory |
| **Follow-up** | Offer one-page home card: memory line + identity pause steps; no homework quiz |

### Session modifications

1. **Shorten** parent source reading to BG 2.13 summary only (5 min instead of 12).
2. **Replace** debate segment with facilitator-led two-option poll for teens.
3. **Provide** glossary slip: deha, dehī, ātmā (soul), kṣetra (field) — four words maximum.
4. **Avoid** assuming prior week vocabulary except "KUTUMBA" and "Kṛṣṇa."
5. **Pair** each newcomer family with a host family for materials and translation if needed.

### What newcomers are not required to accept this week

- Full transmigration mechanics
- Detailed soul qualities or size of the soul
- That suffering is "only illusion" (explicitly rejected — see safeguarding)
- Public gallery display of personal identity work

### Bridge to next visit

If newcomers continue, C1-W3 deepens what the self *is*; this week only establishes what the self is *not* (not identical to the changing body) and how to care for the body in service.
