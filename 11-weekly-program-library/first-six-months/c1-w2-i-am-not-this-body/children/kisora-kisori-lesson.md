# Kiśora–Kiśorī Lesson — I Am Not This Body

**Total time:** 40 minutes  
**Essential question:** What changes in the body, what continues in the self, and how should we act?  
**No forced personal disclosure.** Use anonymized scenarios only.

---

## Core track — ages 9–11

| Time | Activity |
| --- | --- |
| 0–5 min | Opening: write one word for "body" and one for "me" on index cards — collect anonymously |
| 5–15 min | **Text observation** — BG 2.13 (KUTUMBA summary + link) |
| 15–22 min | Body/self comparison diagram (facilitator draws; see visuals) |
| 22–30 min | Social-media identity case (Case CS-01 in research) |
| 30–35 min | Reflective writing (short) |
| 35–40 min | Service-based identity statement + transition |

### Text observation — BG 2.13

Read aloud KUTUMBA summary (not full purport):

> The embodied soul continuously passes through bodily stages — childhood, youth, and old age — just as it already passed through them before. A sober person is not disturbed by this bodily change.

**Observe:** (1) What three stages are named? (2) Who passes through them? (3) What attitude is recommended?

Reference: [vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/13/](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/13/)

### Body/self comparison diagram

Two columns: **Changes** (height, hair, injuries, mood swings) vs. **Continues** (awareness, responsibility, capacity to serve, relationship with Kṛṣṇa). Link to [BG 13.1](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/13/1/) — field vs. knower (brief).

### Social-media case

Facilitator reads anonymized Case CS-01. Pairs answer: mistaken conclusion? compassionate response?

### Reflective writing (3–4 sentences)

Prompt: "When I remember I am not only my appearance, I will…" Optional share; no grading.

### Service identity statement

Template: "I am Kṛṣṇa's servant. This body can be used to ___ this week." One concrete action.

| **CC Madhya 20.108** | One sentence: constitutional identity is eternal service — not just negation |
| **Challenge writing** | One paragraph analyzing a peer pressure situation without naming real persons |

### Debate rules card

1. Attack ideas, not people.  
2. Use "I think" and source reference.  
3. Facilitator may pause for safeguarding.  
4. No recording without consent.

---

## Disability and dignity application

Short facilitator input:

- Bodily limitation does not diminish the soul ([BG 2.20](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/20/) — soul not destroyed when body is injured).
- Mocking or exclusion is never justified by philosophy.
- Practical seva: include, accommodate, speak respectfully.

---

## Facilitator cautions

- Do not debate transmigration details — defer to C1-W3.
- Watch for eating-disorder or self-harm cues; private follow-up per safeguarding policy.
- Never ask youth to rate their appearance publicly.
