# Lāla–Lālī Lesson — I Am Not This Body

**Total time:** 40 minutes (aligns with parent track)  
**Memory line:** "I have a body. I am a soul. I belong to Kṛṣṇa."  
**Sanskrit phrase:** *dehī* — the one inside the body (say: "DAY-hee")  
**No frightening death imagery.** Use life stages only.

---

## Core track — ages 4–6 (default delivery)

| Time | Activity |
| --- | --- |
| 0–5 min | Opening: recall C1-W1 if attended; sing one short song; show today's picture card — baby, child, grown-up |
| 5–15 min | **Story** (below) — 8 minutes + 2 min wonder questions |
| 15–22 min | Costume relay + name card |
| 22–28 min | Body-care sorting game |
| 28–32 min | **Movement break** — "grow from seed to tree to resting" |
| 32–36 min | Thread craft on three-stage drawing |
| 36–40 min | Recall line + hand to heart; transition to [shared-family-transition.md](shared-family-transition.md) |

### Story — "Rādhā's Three Pictures" (8–10 min)

Facilitator tells (with picture cards or drawings):

> Rādhā (a puppet or volunteer child with permission) finds three photos in a box.  
> Picture 1: a tiny baby in a blanket.  
> Picture 2: a girl missing two front teeth, holding a mṛdaṅga.  
> Picture 3: Amma today, with glasses and grey hair.  
> Rādhā laughs: "That baby can't be Amma!"  
> Amma smiles: "The body changed. *I* am still Amma. The body is like clothes that change. *I* am the person who loves you and serves Kṛṣṇa."  
> Rādhā puts her hand on her heart: "I have a body. I am me inside."

**Wonder questions (no wrong answers):**  
- Which picture looks most different?  
- Who stayed Amma?

### Costume relay

One child wears hat → vest → shawl while holding the **same name card**. Group chants: "Outfit changed — [Name] stayed!"

### Body-care sorting

Two baskets: **Good for my body** / **Not good for my body**. Cards: sleep, bath, prasāda, walking, hugging vs. hitting, no sleep, only candy, being mean.

### Movement break — "Body grows"

Crouch (baby) → stand arms wide (child) → slow walk with hands on back (older). Freeze; facilitator: "The body pose changed. You are still you."

### Thread craft

Three boxes drawn on paper (baby, child, older). Yarn thread pierces all three. Label: "Same person."

### Take-home

One small drawing template: child draws self today; parent adds baby photo or stick figure at home if they wish.

## Extension — ages 7–8

Add after story (do not replace core activities):

| Addition | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **BG 2.13 rhythm** | First line in call-and-response (English summary): "The body changes from boyhood to youth to old age — the soul passes through them." Clap pattern 4-4-4. |
| **Scenario cards** | Appearance teasing, winning/losing game, friend with crutches — choose kind words. Emphasize: different body ability does not mean less person. |
| **Deeper recall** | Add: "Not the body means **care** for the body, not ignore it." |
| **Sanskrit** | Repeat *dehī* — "the knower inside the body." |

---

## Facilitator cautions

- Do not invite children to describe trauma or medical details.
- Use generic puppets if family photos are unavailable.
- If a child says "Will I die?" respond: "Kṛṣṇa takes care of you. Today we learn the body changes like clothes. Your Amma/Papa can talk more at home." Redirect to care game.
- Disability example: soul is not "less"; support needs are real — help friend, don't stare.
