## Analogy and Practical Application

> **Scope:** These analogies teach the distinction between changing body and continuing self. They do **not** prove transmigration mechanics, subtle-body cosmology, or full soul ontology — those belong to later modules (especially C1-W3).

### 1. Childhood–youth–old age continuity

| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Source status** | Scriptural — [BG 2.13](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/13/) |
| **Teaching value** | Shows direct experience: the body changes stages, yet the person says "that was me." |
| **Limitation** | Memory and personality also change in expression; the analogy introduces continuity, not full metaphysics. |
| **Age suitability** | All ages; primary for Lāla–Lālī photo timeline |
| **Risk of misuse** | Denying real bodily needs of children or elders ("you'll get another body soon") |
| **Family example** | Spread three family photos; child points to the same person. Parent asks: "What stayed the same even when the body looked different?" |

### 2. Changing garments (vāsāṁsi)

| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Source status** | Scriptural — [BG 2.22](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/2/22/) |
| **Teaching value** | Body is worn, changed, and left — the wearer continues |
| **Limitation** | Garments have no inner life; the soul is conscious. Do not imply the body is worthless. |
| **Age suitability** | 6+; costume relay for 4–8 |
| **Risk of misuse** | Carelessness toward health ("just change bodies"); contempt for disability |
| **Family example** | Volunteer swaps three labeled jackets (baby, student, elder) while holding one name card. "The outfit changed; the person didn't." |

### 3. Driver and vehicle

| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Source status** | KUTUMBA pedagogical analogy (not attributed to Śrīla Prabhupāda — no verified lecture source on file) |
| **Teaching value** | Clarifies agency: the conscious self uses the body as an instrument |
| **Limitation** | Vehicle has no independent will; the soul is responsible. Vehicle damage still matters. |
| **Age suitability** | 9+; simplified for 7–8 with toy car and driver figure |
| **Risk of misuse** | "I'm not responsible for what this body does" — false detachment from ethics |
| **Family example** | Parent asks: "Who is driving when you choose kind words or hurtful words?" Link to body-care: maintain the vehicle for service. |

### 4. Observer and observed

| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Source status** | KUTUMBA pedagogical (aligned with [BG 13.1–3](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/13/1/)) |
| **Teaching value** | You can notice breath, sensation, and thought — therefore you are not identical to them |
| **Limitation** | Observation alone does not establish full Vedāntic conclusions; keep scope modest |
| **Age suitability** | 10+; gentle breath-noticing for 7–8 in identity pause |
| **Risk of misuse** | Spiritual bypassing — using "I am the observer" to avoid grief or medical care |
| **Family example** | During identity pause: "Notice the breath. Who notices?" Three slow breaths before chanting. |

### 5. House and resident

| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Source status** | KUTUMBA pedagogical (field/knower theme from BG 13) |
| **Teaching value** | The body is where the self lives; maintenance and respect remain necessary |
| **Limitation** | Residents move houses; transmigration details belong to C1-W3 |
| **Age suitability** | 4–8 story format; 9–14 diagram |
| **Family example** | "We clean and repair our home so service can happen. The body is Kṛṣṇa's instrument — care for it." |

### 6. Device and user (modern pedagogical)

| Field | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Source status** | KUTUMBA-created — marked modern analogy |
| **Teaching value** | Helps teens separate online avatar/appearance from the continuing person |
| **Limitation** | Devices lack consciousness; do not reduce the soul to a "user account" |
| **Age suitability** | 12–14 primarily |
| **Risk of misuse** | Minimizing cyberbullying harm ("it's just pixels") |
| **Family example** | Compare profile photo edits to costume changes. "The account picture changed; you are still the person Kṛṣṇa knows." |
