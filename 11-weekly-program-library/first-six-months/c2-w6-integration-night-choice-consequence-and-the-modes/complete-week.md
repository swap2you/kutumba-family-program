## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| A family has a recurring Friday problem. Everyone arrives late. The parent blames traffic, the children blame hunger, and another adult says, ‘This is our karma; nothing can change.’ The home is rushed, devices remain active and no one prepared food on time. By the time kīrtana begins, irritation has already spread. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Explain the five-lens case method: action, choice, conditioning/modes, false promise and devotional shelter.

1. 5–25 min — Family case rounds. Each table receives two cases and prepares a three-minute response.

1. 25–32 min — Cross-table review: another family identifies one strength and one missing lens.

1. 32–36 min — Verse recognition relay without speed ranking.

1. 36–40 min — Select one continuing method and one measurable environmental change.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Puppet case: rushed morning. Children choose one helpful seed, use traffic-light pause and tidy the space.

- Mode-weather matching with actions that clear the sky.

- Bedtime remembrance demonstration.

- Choose one tool card to take home.

### Age 7-9

- Solve illustrated cases using action, result, choice and helpful environment.

- Verse picture match: seed, rudder, clothes, filters and shelter.

- Role-play a respectful redo after a poor choice.

- Create a family tool wheel.

### Age 10-13

- Facilitate a case using the five-lens method.

- Identify victim-blaming, fatalism and mode-labeling in sample statements.

- Design a realistic intervention for a digital attention loop.

- Give one evidence-based reason for the selected family method.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- Which verse and analogy belong to each Cycle 2 lesson?

- What factors are present in the case?

- What action can be changed now?

### Understanding

- Why is one-lens analysis usually weak?

- How do modes and free will interact?

- Why must surrender include practical boundaries and responsible action?

### Application

- Which Cycle 2 method produced the clearest benefit?

- What condition must change for it to continue?

- Who will support the plan without policing?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Five-Lens Family Case Lab

1. Lens 1: What action and likely reaction are present?

1. Lens 2: What is controlled, influenced or not controlled?

1. Lens 3: Which mode-like conditions are active?

1. Lens 4: What false promise or attention loop is operating?

1. Lens 5: What responsible action and devotional shelter should be used next?

1. Families present in three minutes; facilitator corrects philosophy after the presentation, not during it.

## Teacher Preparation

- Prepare at least six realistic cases with no recent family-specific details.

- Create facilitator answer guides with acceptable ranges, not one scripted solution.

- Review prohibited responses: karma diagnosis, victim blame, misogyny, child shaming and deterministic labels.

- Arrange mixed-age stations and ensure youth are not left as sole supervisors.

- Prepare continuity cards and private follow-up list.

## Assessment

- Score privately on five lenses, compassion and practicality.

- Secure = uses at least four lenses without a major misconception; developing = two or three; support = major misconception or no actionable plan.

- Children demonstrate tools through role-play, matching or explanation.

- No winning table or public score.

## Cycle outcomes

- Families understand Kṛṣṇa as the supreme enjoyer, proprietor, friend and source.

- Families can support Kṛṣṇa’s supreme position from primary sources without sectarian aggression.

- Families use guru, sādhu and śāstra with inquiry, verification and safety boundaries.

- Families participate in attentive, non-competitive kīrtana.

- Families recognize the nine processes and establish a realistic thirty-day bhakti baseline.
