# C2-W4 — The Three Modes of Material Nature

## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| The same family can experience three very different evenings. On one evening, the room is reasonably clean, the meal is timely, voices are calm and a short reading feels possible. On another, everyone is rushing, several screens are active, plans keep changing and irritation rises. On a third, dishes remain for days, people oversleep, avoid responsibility and feel too dull to begin anything. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Observe three room images: calm/ordered, overstimulated and neglected. Describe conditions, not people.

1. 5–14 min — Read BG 14.5–9 in summarized form. Build a three-column tendency chart.

1. 14–20 min — Explain mixing and change; discuss how fatigue, media, food, company and time of day influence behavior.

1. 20–29 min — Scenario analysis: study session, temple service, shopping, family argument and weekend sleep. Identify dominant influences and one elevating action.

1. 29–35 min — Goodness trap: pride, comfort attachment and judging others. Connect to BG 14.26.

1. 35–40 min — Redesign one evening routine using remove, add, move earlier and simplify.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Three weather faces: clear sky, windy storm and dark fog. Match simple behaviors without calling children a mode.

- Room reset game: tidy three items, lower noise and sit for one chant.

- Food picture discussion focused on freshness and moderation, not shame.

- Recall: ‘I can choose habits that help me hear Kṛṣṇa.’

### Age 7-9

- Mode detectives: identify clues in short stories and suggest one better choice.

- Colored-filter lamp demonstration.

- Create a before-bed clarity checklist: tidy, wash, reduce screen, chant, sleep.

- Discuss why people can change and should not be labeled.

### Age 10-13

- Read BG 14.5 and summarize each mode in one sentence.

- Analyze an algorithm-driven media feed for passion and ignorance influences.

- Discuss how goodness can create superiority.

- Design a two-hour evening environment that supports school duties and bhakti.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- What three modes are named in BG 14.5?

- What tendencies are associated with each?

- What does BG 14.26 say about transcending the modes?

### Understanding

- Why should modes describe influences rather than fixed people?

- How can goodness still bind?

- Why is environmental design spiritually relevant?

### Application

- Which input most agitates your home?

- Which neglected condition makes practice harder?

- What will you remove, add, move earlier or simplify?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Clarity Before Chanting

1. Compare a noisy, cluttered setup with a simple clean setup.

1. Families perform a five-minute reset: put away items, wash hands, silence devices and arrange seating.

1. Lead three minutes of attentive mahā-mantra response chanting.

1. Reflect: which physical changes altered attention?

1. Clarify that external goodness supports bhakti but is not itself pure devotion.

## Teacher Preparation

- Read BG 14.5–9, 14.17–18 and 14.26 with purports.

- Prepare mode descriptions as tendencies, not personality tests.

- Avoid cultural, gender, class or mental-health stereotypes.

- Use food examples cautiously; do not police allergies, medical diets or family budgets.

- Prepare room images, safe colored filters and reset materials.
