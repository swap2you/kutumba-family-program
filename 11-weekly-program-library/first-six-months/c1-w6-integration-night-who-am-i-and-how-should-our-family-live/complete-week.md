## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| A family begins a road journey. One child keeps asking, ‘Are we there yet?’ Another becomes attached to every rest stop and refuses to leave. A third complains that the car is not as nice as another family’s car. The parents also forget the map and start arguing about who is responsible. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Explain non-competitive rules and the appreciation-only response format.

1. 5–15 min — Mixed-family retrieval stations: verse match, analogy match and misconception repair.

1. 15–30 min — Three-minute family presentations or displays. Maximum four families in this block; remaining families present during lab stations.

1. 30–36 min — Adult case lightning round: appearance crisis, schedule pressure and canceled plan. Each response must include identity + action.

1. 36–40 min — Private continuity decision: start, continue, simplify or stop one home practice.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Costume parade showing one child under changing hats or scarves.

- Picture stations: body changes, soul stays, human hands serve, bubbles are temporary.

- Group chant with one child holding each word card of the memory line.

- Choose a sticker for one practice to continue; no prize ranking.

### Age 7-9

- Mini-drama: traveler, vehicle, map and rest stop.

- Four-corner quiz with explanation after each answer.

- Create a cycle wheel connecting the four principles.

- Present one home-practice report: what helped, what interrupted and what will change.

### Age 10-13

- Facilitate one station or present a two-minute evidence–analogy–application explanation.

- Misconception repair cards: ‘not the body means health does not matter,’ ‘temporary means useless,’ and ‘all souls are God.’

- Peer appreciation using specific language: ‘Your example helped me understand…’

- Write one question to carry into the karma cycle.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- Which verse belongs to each principle?

- Which analogy clarified the difference between body and soul?

- Which home practice was actually completed?

### Understanding

- How do the four lessons form one sequence?

- Which common misconception is most dangerous in family life?

- Why is honest simplification better than impressive failure?

### Application

- Which practice will your family carry forward?

- What trigger and minimum version will protect it?

- What support do you need from the group without surrendering family responsibility?

## Teacher Preparation

- Collect presentation format choices one week earlier; limit preparation burden to twenty minutes per family.

- Set up four stations with answer keys and age adaptations.

- Prepare a visible rule: no ranking, no scores announced, no correction during a child’s performance unless safety requires it.

- Assign timekeeper, station leads and child-transition support.

- Review Cycle 1 misconceptions and prepare concise corrections with references.

## Assessment

- Use a four-domain rubric: recall, explanation, application and teamwork.

- Record each family as secure, developing or needs a mentor check-in; do not publish results.

- Facilitator identifies which Cycle 1 concept requires a five-minute review at the start of Cycle 2.

## Cycle outcomes

- Families understand karma without fatalism or victim-blaming.

- Families practice responsible next-choice skills under real constraints.

- Families can discuss reincarnation calmly and compassionately.

- Families establish one boundary plus devotional shelter against an attention trap.
