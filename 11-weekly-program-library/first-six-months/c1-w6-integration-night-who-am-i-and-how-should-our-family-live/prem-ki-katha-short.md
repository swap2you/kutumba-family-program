# Prem-kī-Kathā (Short) — Cycle 1 Integration

## 6. Source-grounded narrative

_[Compressed integration recollection — not a new principal līlā]_

Families return to the forest of inquiry with quieter hearts. Six weeks ago the question was fresh: what is worth hearing with this human life? Through body and soul, human opportunity, and the temporary world's call, each week offered one source-grounded scene — sages at Naimiṣāraṇya, Arjuna's sorrow met by Kṛṣṇa's compassion, Jaḍa Bharata's patient instruction, Nārada's mercy to Mṛgāri, Dhruva's transformed aspiration. Tonight is not another distant līlā to memorize. It is **recollection and offering**: what changed in our hearing, our habits, our family rhythm?

Integration means honest synthesis. Parents may name one charter purpose that became real — protected Friday hearing, gentler speech, a child who remembers a memory line. Youth may name one verse link they verified before sharing. No competitive ranking. No forced testimony. KUTUMBA is a disciplined community, not a performance stage.

**Source anchors:** Cycle 1 module narratives (paraphrase only); SB 1.2.18 (steady hearing); KUTUMBA charter purposes — governance citation for institutional claims.

The turning point is gratitude that turns into practice — one family commitment written for the week ahead.
