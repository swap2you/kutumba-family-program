# C1-W3 — The Nature of the Soul

*Cycle 1 — Identity and the Human Problem*

| **Field** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- |
| **Week code** | C1-W3 |
| **Cycle function** | Scriptural principle and application |
| **Purpose** | Move beyond the negative statement ‘not the body’ to a positive understanding of the jīva as eternal, conscious, individual, minute and naturally related to Kṛṣṇa through service. |
| **Key verse** | Bhagavad-gītā 2.20 — ajo nityaḥ śāśvato ’yaṁ purāṇo |
| **Memory line** | The soul is unborn, eternal and not destroyed when the body is destroyed. |
| **Bhakti laboratory** | Service Intention and Cleanliness Seva |
| **Minimum home practice** | Offer one routine action to Kṛṣṇa daily with a ten-second intention. |

## Learning Outcomes

| **Outcome domain** | **By the end of the session, participants should be able to…** |
| --- | --- |
| **Knowledge** | Describe five qualities taught in the assigned texts: eternal, conscious, individual, unchanging through bodily change and a minute part of Kṛṣṇa. |
| **Skill** | Support a statement about the soul with a verse reference rather than imagination. |
| **Practice** | Offer one daily action consciously as service rather than for ego or recognition. |
| **Character** | Dignity, responsibility, service identity and respect for every living being. |
| **Child** | Say that every living being has a soul and should be treated with care. |

## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| Last week we learned that the changing body is not the complete self. But merely saying what we are not leaves an empty space. A child may ask, ‘Then what am I?’ Bhagavad-gītā gives a positive answer. The soul is not a temporary blank or a piece of matter. The soul is an eternal, conscious individual. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Retrieval: in pairs, state one correct and one incorrect conclusion from ‘I am not this body.’

1. 5–13 min — Guided reading of BG 2.20. Mark words describing what the soul is and what the soul is not.

1. 13–20 min — Compare BG 15.7 and BG 13.23: individual jīva, material nature and the Supreme witness. Keep terminology introductory.

1. 20–28 min — Analogy lab: spark/fire, sunray/sun and hand/body. Groups identify what each analogy helps explain and where it can mislead.

1. 28–35 min — Case: a person says, ‘All souls are spiritual, so I do not need authority or service.’ Prepare a text-grounded response.

1. 35–40 min — Service reframing: select one disliked duty and rewrite its intention as service without pretending the duty is pleasant.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Light demonstration: one lamp can light several battery candles; each light is real but not the source lamp.

- Living/not-living sort using pictures; emphasize care for living beings.

- Service-body actions: hands can clean, feet can visit temple, voice can chant, ears can hear.

- Recall: ‘I am an eternal soul, a tiny part of Kṛṣṇa.’

### Age 7-9

- Five-quality shield: eternal, conscious, individual, tiny part of Kṛṣṇa, meant for service.

- Analogy check: what is similar and different between a spark and a fire?

- Compassion scenarios involving insects, pets, siblings and elderly persons.

- Practice the key Sanskrit phrase with call-and-response.

### Age 10-13

- Close reading: list claims in BG 2.20 and compare with BG 15.7.

- Discuss why ‘part of Kṛṣṇa’ does not mean the individual soul is equal in power to Kṛṣṇa.

- Analyze dignity plus boundaries: respecting the soul does not require accepting harmful behavior.

- Choose one invisible service to complete without seeking credit.

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- Which qualities of the soul are stated in BG 2.20?

- What relationship is stated in BG 15.7?

- What symptom indicates the presence of the soul in Śrīla Prabhupāda’s explanation?

### Understanding

- What does qualitative similarity mean?

- Why does individuality continue in devotional philosophy?

- Why is service compatible with spiritual dignity?

### Application

- Which daily duty can you reconnect to Kṛṣṇa?

- How can you show dignity to a difficult person while keeping a boundary?

- How can children serve without being overburdened?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Service Intention and Cleanliness Seva

1. Demonstrate how to prepare a small worship or classroom area: gather, sort, wipe, return and inspect.

1. Assign age-appropriate tasks with adult oversight; no chemical handling by young children.

1. Before starting, everyone silently states the service intention.

1. Afterward, discuss the difference between finishing quickly, seeking praise and serving carefully.

## Teacher Preparation

- Read BG 2.17–25, 15.7 and 13.23 with purports; use exact terminology and avoid monistic interpretations.

- Prepare a safe LED light demonstration rather than open flame for children.

- Check cleaning materials and child-safe task assignments.

- Brief facilitators that service does not justify domestic unfairness, coercion or abuse.

- Prepare glossary cards: jīva, ātmā, Paramātmā, prakṛti and sevā.
