## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Time snapshot: mark yesterday’s waking hours in broad categories without names or public sharing.

1. 5–12 min — Read the memory line from ŚB 11.9.29 and identify three ideas: rare, temporary, value-giving.

1. 12–18 min — Read a short summary of ŚB 7.6.1. Discuss formation in childhood without imposing adult standards.

1. 18–28 min — ‘Someday’ case study: work pressure, children’s activities, elder care and screens leave no protected spiritual time. Groups design a realistic fifteen-minute appointment.

1. 28–34 min — Question workshop: convert shallow or argumentative questions into inquiry that can guide practice.

1. 34–40 min — Calendar the family appointment and identify one likely interruption plus backup plan.
