# C2-W2 — Free Will and Responsibility: The Next Choice Matters

*Cycle 2 — Karma, Rebirth and Material Nature*

| **Field** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- |
| **Week code** | C2-W2 |
| **Cycle function** | Scriptural story and application |
| **Purpose** | Teach that the conditioned soul does not control every circumstance but retains meaningful, minute choice and responsibility, especially in how one responds to guidance, habits and present conditions. |
| **Key verse** | Bhagavad-gītā 18.63 — vimṛśyaitad aśeṣeṇa yathecchasi tathā kuru |
| **Memory line** | After giving knowledge, Kṛṣṇa tells Arjuna to deliberate fully and then act as he chooses. |
| **Bhakti laboratory** | Pause–Remember–Choose Practice |
| **Minimum home practice** | Use pause–remember–choose once daily on the chosen trigger. |

## Learning Outcomes

| **Outcome domain** | **By the end of the session, participants should be able to…** |
| --- | --- |
| **Knowledge** | Explain that material nature, prior conditioning, the body and other agents limit control. |
| **Skill** | Separate what is controlled, influenced and not controlled in a difficult situation. |
| **Practice** | Apply the three-step choice pause to one predictable trigger each day. |
| **Character** | Responsibility, courage, patience and willingness to seek guidance. |
| **Child** | Understand that feelings can be strong while choices still matter. |

## Prem-kī-Kathā Script

*Delivery target: 8–10 minutes of scripted narration plus 5–7 minutes of interaction. Do not exceed the 20-minute block.*

| Two statements often appear in the same home. First: ‘I had no choice; you made me angry.’ Second: ‘I should be able to control everything if I am spiritual.’ Both are inaccurate. We do not control every circumstance, body, emotion, other person or result. But we are not merely helpless machines either. |
| --- |

## Parent Lesson — 40 Minutes

1. 0–5 min — Control circle: write one current concern and sort elements into control, influence and no control.

1. 5–12 min — Read BG 18.63 and 18.73. Note instruction, deliberation, choice and willing alignment.

1. 12–18 min — Brief reading of BG 3.33–34. Discuss conditioning without excusing harmful behavior.

1. 18–28 min — Trigger chain: event → body sensation → thought → urge → choice → consequence. Map a low-risk example.

1. 28–35 min — Practice pause–remember–choose in pairs using prepared scenarios.

1. 35–40 min — Select one trigger and design an environmental support: reminder card, delay, device boundary or help request.

## Children’s Parallel Lesson — 40 Minutes

*Common child timing: 0–5 opening/recall; 5–15 story or text; 15–28 movement, art, game or investigation; 28–35 verse/recall; 35–40 cleanup and family transition.*

### Age 4-6

- Traffic-light routine: red stop, yellow remember Kṛṣṇa and the rule, green choose a safe action.

- Puppet scenario: toy taken, strong anger, two possible choices.

- Body signal game: identify tight fists, loud voice and fast breathing as signals to pause.

- Practice asking an adult for help.

### Age 7-9

- Arjuna choice story: confused, asks Kṛṣṇa, hears, thinks and acts.

- Control/influence/no-control sorting with school and family situations.

- Traffic-light role plays with a respectful redo after an unhelpful choice.

- Create a pocket pause card.

### Age 10-13

- Read BG 18.63 and discuss whether choice means ‘do whatever you feel.’

- Map one digital habit trigger and identify the smallest point of intervention.

- Discuss coercion, peer pressure and when to seek a trusted adult rather than manage alone.

- Write a choice statement: ‘I cannot control ___; I can choose ___.’

## Questions: Discovery, Understanding and Application

### Discovery

- What does Kṛṣṇa tell Arjuna to do before choosing?

- What factors influence conditioned behavior?

- What choice does Arjuna make in BG 18.73?

### Understanding

- Why is responsibility not the same as controlling every result?

- How can conditioning explain behavior without excusing harm?

- Why does good guidance strengthen rather than remove freedom?

### Application

- What trigger repeatedly narrows your choices?

- What signal tells you to pause?

- What is one safe next choice and one support you will use?

## Bhakti Laboratory — Pause–Remember–Choose Practice

1. Teach a physical pause signal: hand to heart or touching a pocket card.

1. Remember one line: ‘I am Kṛṣṇa’s servant; choose truth and care.’

1. Offer two or three acceptable next actions for each scenario.

1. Role-play once, receive one correction and repeat successfully.

1. For unsafe situations, the correct choice is immediate adult help or leaving—not quiet endurance.

| **Evidence** | Private three-line log; no one must reveal sensitive family conflict. |

## Teacher Preparation

- Read BG 18.61–63, 18.73, 3.33–34 and 5.14–15 with purports.

- Prepare low-risk scenarios; do not invite public disclosure of abuse, addiction or severe conflict.

- Brief volunteers on mandatory safety escalation: a child’s request for help is not merely a self-regulation exercise.

- Prepare traffic-light cards and control-circle sheets.

- Avoid presenting free will as unlimited independence from Kṛṣṇa or nature.
