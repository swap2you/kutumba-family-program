# Prem-kī-Kathā — The fish in the well

## 1. Katha title

**The fish in the well** — SB 7.5 — limited vision within māyā's covering

## 2. Module connection

This katha supports **Māyā: Decorating the Prison Cell** and the key verse [7.14](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/7/14/).

**Memory line:** Kṛṣṇa's material energy is difficult to overcome, but those who surrender to Him can cross beyond it.

**Scope boundary:** BG 7.14, 2.62–63, 15.7, 9.10; prison-cell analogy; attention boundaries. Exclusions: Misogynistic 'women are māyā'; world-hatred; diagnosing addiction publicly

## 4. Setting and devotional mood

_[Facilitator transition — quiet room, families seated together]_

A person receives a notification while reading Bhagavad-gītā. 'I will check for ten seconds,' the mind says. Twenty minutes later, the person has moved through messages, news, shopping, and comparison...

The mood is **sober curiosity** — families willing to hear what Kṛṣṇa and the ācārya paramparā teach without sensationalism.

## 6. Source-grounded narrative

_[Source narrative / paraphrase — not invented direct quotes]_

**Paraphrase (source-grounded):** Prahlāda Mahārāja taught that persons absorbed only in family, shop, and neighborhood comfort may mistake a small well for the whole ocean — like a fish who has never seen the sea.

**Paraphrase (source-grounded):** The covering of māyā is not that matter is unreal in every sense; it is that misplaced attachment makes us forget Kṛṣṇa and our actual interest.

**Paraphrase (source-grounded):** Crossing māyā comes through surrender to Kṛṣṇa and devotional shelter — not hatred of the world, not demeaning women, and not mere white-knuckle control without replacement practice.

## 7. Turning point

The turning point is **willingness to hear and adjust** — not perfect control of every result. Families turn toward Kṛṣṇa, śāstra, and practical repair.

## 8. Central teaching

Kṛṣṇa's material energy is difficult to overcome, but those who surrender to Him can cross beyond it.
