## Rationale

The original three-band model (4–6, 7–9, 10–13) treated developmental differences too coarsely. Operational delivery uses two groups with internal differentiation:

| Group | Ages | Internal bands |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Lāla–Lālī** | 4–8 | Core 4–6; extension 7–8 |
| **Kiśora–Kiśorī** | 9–14 | Core 9–11; challenge 12–14 |

Parents and adults remain one track. All groups study the same central truth.

## Developmental differences

- Ages 4–6: story, movement, simple recall, no frightening imagery
- Ages 7–8: sequencing, sorting, short verse rhythm
- Ages 9–11: observation, respectful discussion, bounded writing
- Ages 12–14: case analysis, debate with facilitator guardrails, optional leadership of short segments

## Mixed-age teaching

- Same theme as parents; separate space when possible
- Reunite for `children/shared-family-transition.md` (10 minutes)
- Two adults minimum in visible child space

## Teen leadership boundaries

Teens may lead bounded presentation or service tasks. They may **not** become responsible for child safety, discipline, or confidential safeguarding conversations.

## Safeguarding

- No forced disclosure
- Route trauma, eating disorders, abuse topics to parents privately
- See `04-children-youth/CHILDREN-YOUTH-FORMATION-OPERATING-MODEL.md`
