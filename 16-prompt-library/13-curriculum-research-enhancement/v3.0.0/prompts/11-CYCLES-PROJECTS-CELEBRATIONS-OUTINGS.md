# Prompt 11 — Cycles, Projects, Celebrations, and Relational Outings

## Objective

Preserve the six-module theological sequence while adding rhythm, relationships, joy, project work, rest, and celebration.

## Calendar principle

`C1-W1` etc. remain stable module IDs. They are not mandatory consecutive calendar weeks.

## Recommended cycle arc

1. Modules 1–5: concept and application
2. Module 6: integration, project completion, rehearsal, and assessment
3. Complete off week
4. Cycle Utsava / Mela / showcase
5. Begin next cycle when families and calendar are ready

Outings or temple festivals may occur between modules. They replace rather than overload the normal program.

The annual 40-active-week model remains controlling. Create a calendar impact analysis before adding six separate celebration weeks.

## Cycle project

At the start of each cycle, families choose or receive a bounded project.

The project grows across six modules.

Possible formats:

- drama;
- puppet presentation;
- family poster;
- model;
- gallery;
- quiz;
- short video;
- devotional art;
- story retelling;
- service demonstration;
- song or śloka presentation;
- parent-child dialogue;
- research mini-presentation.

## Project design requirements

- same central theme as the cycle;
- age-appropriate;
- no expensive materials;
- no ranking families;
- no forced public disclosure;
- no child responsible for safety;
- clear weekly micro-task;
- family and individual options;
- accessible presentation format;
- source list;
- rights-cleared media;
- rehearsal plan;
- rubric emphasizing understanding, application, cooperation, and devotional mood.

## Celebration structure

Create a 2.5–3 hour cycle celebration template:

- welcome and kīrtana;
- short cycle remembrance;
- Lāla–Lālī presentations;
- Kiśora–Kiśorī presentations;
- family projects;
- one drama or puppet item;
- gallery walk or quiz;
- guest-safe explanation;
- dancing or joyful kīrtana;
- prasāda potluck;
- gratitude and close.

Do not reveal:

- private home-practice records;
- assessment scores;
- family struggles;
- mentor notes;
- internal review comments.

## Festival alignment

Create a candidate map aligning each cycle celebration with the locally applicable temple calendar when practical.

Do not hard-code festival dates.

A festival may host the showcase only when the devotional focus remains coherent and temple coordination is complete.

## Relational outings

Design low-pressure relationship-building replacements such as:

- picnic;
- nature walk;
- cow protection visit;
- temple service;
- family games;
- cultural visit;
- carnival or community event with a simple devotional opening and closing;
- prasāda gathering.

Do not force a full lecture into every outing.

Every outing must still have:

- one devotional anchor;
- safety plan;
- parent responsibility;
- attendance and transport boundary;
- no overload;
- short reflection.
