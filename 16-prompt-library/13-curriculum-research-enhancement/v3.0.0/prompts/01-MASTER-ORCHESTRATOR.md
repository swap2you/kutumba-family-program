## Mission

Upgrade all 18 first-six-month module folders from extracted structural packs into thoroughly researched, source-traceable, age-banded, visually planned, facilitator-usable learning packages.

The target is not maximum volume. The target is:

- bona fide source control;
- doctrinal precision;
- child and adult comprehension;
- practical family application;
- devotional warmth;
- correct boundaries;
- reusable visual and presentation specifications;
- evidence-backed validation.

## Non-negotiable distinction

Create two layers.

### Layer A — Deep Reference Layer

This may be extensive. It serves researchers, reviewers, and facilitators.

It includes:

- source map;
- claim register;
- verse/reference study notes;
- lecture/media indexes;
- misconceptions;
- contemporary applications;
- analogies;
- FAQs;
- visual specifications;
- bibliography;
- review records.

### Layer B — Live Delivery Layer

This must remain teachable in the available session.

It includes:

- one essential question;
- one controlling principle;
- one or two anchor verses;
- one story or Prem-kī-Kathā;
- one parent learning sequence;
- one Lāla–Lālī lesson;
- one Kiśora–Kiśorī lesson;
- one family laboratory;
- one home practice;
- one assessment;
- one closing reflection.

Do not dump the research dossier into the live lesson.

## Module versus calendar week

Keep IDs such as `C1-W2` for repository stability, but treat them as **module IDs**, not promises of consecutive calendar weeks.

A six-module cycle may span more than six calendar weeks because of:

- family travel;
- temple festivals;
- relational outings;
- service days;
- planned breaks;
- weather;
- review or rehearsal needs.

Do not alter the theological sequence merely to meet a date.
