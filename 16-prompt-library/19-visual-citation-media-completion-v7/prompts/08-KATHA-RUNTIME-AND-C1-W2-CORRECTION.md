# Prompt 08 — Kathā Runtime and C1-W2 Correction

## Objective

Align the narrative standard with the intended live experience.

## Current contradiction

V6 marks 730–893 narrative words as meeting standard, but its own estimate is roughly 5–8 minutes. The intended Prem-kī-Kathā block is approximately 15–20 minutes including interaction.

C1-W2 is called the gold pilot but has only about 263 words in the actual narrative section.

## Adopt explicit timing standards

### Standard teaching week

- source-grounded narration: target 1,350–1,800 words;
- expected live narration: approximately 10–15 minutes depending on language, pauses and interaction;
- child/family interaction: 3–5 minutes;
- total Prem-kī-Kathā block: 15–20 minutes.

Do not pad prose to reach word count. Story integrity and devotional mood control.

### Integration week

- meaningful source-grounded recollection/synthesis: 400–800 words;
- total interactive block may still be 15–20 minutes through stations, recall, music or family offerings;
- label as integration, not full principal-līlā narration.

### Short-form option

Create an explicitly separate 5–7-minute version for late starts or compressed schedules. Never confuse it with the standard version.

## C1-W2 decision

Choose one based on actual editorial review:

1. Deepen C1-W2 into a full standard kathā while preserving its strong structure; or
2. Rename its role to `gold-structure-reference` and stop claiming it is the gold narrative-depth pilot.

Preferred outcome: deepen it carefully around Arjuna’s grief, surrender to instruction and Kṛṣṇa’s compassionate teaching without graphic war narration or invented dialogue.

## Cycle 1

Review C1-W1 and C1-W3–W5. Expand only where meaningful story beats, source context and devotional resolution are missing. Do not insert generic filler.

Create both:

- `prem-ki-katha.md` — standard version;
- `prem-ki-katha-short.md` — controlled short version.

Update facilitator timing and Gamma notes accordingly.

## Validator changes

Narrative validator must report:

- standard narrative words;
- short-form words;
- estimated timing range;
- unique source-grounded events;
- repeated boilerplate;
- story arc completeness;
- exception type;
- human review.

Word count alone cannot pass the module.

## Deliverables

- standard and short forms for Cycle 1;
