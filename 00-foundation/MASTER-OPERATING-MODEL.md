Your clarifications resolve the remaining ambiguity. KUTUMBA should have a complete, version-controlled curriculum architecture from the beginning, but only the first six months need to be developed into detailed weekly lesson packs before launch.

The program will prepare families for stronger sādhana, service, formal śāstric study and, where appropriate, eventual initiation under the proper guru and ISKCON authorities. KUTUMBA itself will not confer initiation, promise initiation or replace Bhakti Śāstrī. It will build the habits, knowledge, character and service culture needed to approach those stages responsibly.

**KUTUMBA Master Operating Model**

**1. Identity**

**KUTUMBA**
*Families Growing in Krishna Consciousness*

**Vision**

To cultivate families whose homes, relationships, habits and service are increasingly centred on Kṛṣṇa.

**Mission**

KUTUMBA helps parents and children grow together through:

śravaṇam — hearing

kīrtanam — chanting and glorification

smaraṇam and mananam — remembering and reflecting

arcanam and ācāra — worship and devotional conduct

sevā — practical service

sādhu-saṅga — meaningful association

utsava and yātrā — festivals, temple visits and pilgrimage

family care — strong, safe relationships across generations

The program therefore combines philosophy with practical household worship, service, cleanliness, chanting, pilgrimage and family culture. Śrīla Prabhupāda specifically connects householder advancement with family worship, practical arcana, kīrtana, temple cleansing, scriptural instruction and visiting holy places under proper guidance.

**2. The Six Charter Purposes**

These should appear in the charter, orientation material, website and annual presentation.

**Purpose 1 — Establish K****ṛ****ṣ****ṇ****a-centred homes**

Help each participating family develop a practical home culture that includes chanting, hearing, offering food, prayer, worship, cleanliness and service.

**Purpose 2 — Provide systematic spiritual education**

Teach sambandha, abhidheya and prayojana progressively through Śrīla Prabhupāda’s books and authorised Gauḍīya Vaiṣṇava teachings.

**Purpose 3 — Develop devotional character**

Cultivate humility, responsibility, truthfulness, compassion, discipline, cleanliness, punctuality, cooperation and protection from Vaiṣṇava-aparādha.

**Purpose 4 — Strengthen parents and children together**

Parents should not outsource spiritual education to a weekly teacher. Children should see their parents learning, chanting, serving and improving alongside them.

**Purpose 5 — Connect families with temple, festivals, service and dhāma**

KUTUMBA should increase—not reduce—meaningful temple participation. Families should attend Ratha-yātrā, Janmāṣṭamī and other festivals, visit places such as Gītā Nāgarī, serve cows, participate in pilgrimages and learn the proper mood of visiting sacred places.

**Purpose 6 — Prepare participants for progressive commitment**

Help participants become ready for:

steady personal sādhana

deeper service

Bhakti Steps or comparable gradual development

formal Bhakti Śāstrī study

approaching dīkṣā under the proper authority

eventual leadership and teaching service, where qualified

Preparation for initiation should be understood as gradual formation—not merely completing lessons. Caitanya-caritāmṛta describes preliminary preparation through ārati, śāstra classes, regulative principles, devotee association and sustained practice; chanting remains available immediately, while formal initiation comes under proper guidance and qualification.

**3. Non-negotiable operating principles**

**K****ṛ****ṣ****ṇ****a remains at the centre**

Picnics, kayaking, trekking, ice cream gatherings and games are allowed, but they are not ordinary social outings.

Every KUTUMBA activity includes at least a simple spiritual centre:

10–20 minutes of kīrtana

japa walk

Kṛṣṇa-kathā or story

gratitude prayer

service

festival remembrance

discussion related to the location

The activity remains enjoyable without becoming materially centred.

**No competition in prasāda, singing or service**

There is no comparison of:

who cooked more

who brought expensive dishes

whose child knows more

who sings better

who hosts the best

who chants more rounds

Growth is measured privately against one’s own previous practice.

**No improvisation in philosophy or worship standards**

Exact ārati procedures, mantras, bhoga offerings and Deity-worship practices must come from authorised ISKCON standards and qualified guidance. Śrīla Prabhupāda warned against manufacturing new worship processes or allowing pūjārīs to act independently.

**No gossip or faction-building**

Discussion of another family’s weaknesses, marriage, children, finances, temple relationships or private spiritual practice is prohibited unless it is part of confidential care by an authorised leader.

Temple politics do not enter the Friday program.

**No passive spectatorship**

Every family gradually receives service. However, participation begins after training, not immediately.

**No overloading**

During the first year:

one principal program per week

outings and festivals replace that week’s regular gathering

no expectation of multiple KUTUMBA events every week

planned breaks are protected

family emergencies are handled with maturity

**9. Service roster**

The first month is training. No inexperienced family should be handed a ritual or operational role without preparation.

**Phase 1 — Weeks 1–4: demonstration**

Core team performs all major roles while families observe and practise.

**Phase 2 — Weeks 5–8: shadow service**

Each family assists a trained person.

**Phase 3 — Cycle 2 onward: full rotation**

For six families, use six service stations:

| **Station** | **Main responsibility** |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Prasāda Primary | Prepare the standard meal |
| 2. Operations Backup | Emergency food support, setup and warming equipment |
| 3. Bhoga and Worship | Bhoga plate, altar readiness and authorised offering support |
| 4. Kīrtana and Ārati | Approved songs, instruments and ārati preparation |
| 5. Welcome and Children | Attendance, welcome, classroom materials and child transition |
| 6. Serving and Cleanup | Serving, dishes, floors, waste and equipment reset |

The assignments rotate weekly.

**Important separation**

The cooking family does not clean that week.

The kīrtana family does not disappear into the kitchen.

No food preparation occurs during kathā.

Everything arrives cooked and ready for warming. The bhoga team handles the offering at the predetermined time. Only a designated person may briefly attend to food safety or warming equipment.

**10. Prasāda model for 20–25 people**

One family can prepare a simple meal for this size when the menu is controlled.

**Standard menu**

Choose only three principal preparations:

rice or another grain

dal or suitable protein preparation

one vegetable preparation

Optional:

fruit

simple salad

one festival sweet when scheduled

Do not add five extra dishes.

**Weekly process**

**Sunday or Monday:** roster reminder
**Tuesday:** primary family confirms
**Wednesday:** headcount and allergies confirmed
**Thursday:** final quantities shared
**Friday:** food arrives at the agreed time

**Backup family**

The backup family does not cook a second full meal.

It should be ready to supply one simple emergency option, such as:

khichadi

rice

fruit

simple sabji

approved purchased ingredient or preparation, where appropriate

If the primary family cancels early, the backup assumes the full simplified menu and is removed from its other assignment.

**Required documents**

standard recipe and quantity sheet

kitchen-cleanliness checklist

bhoga handoff checklist

serving checklist

cleanup checklist

Food for Kṛṣṇa is to be prepared and offered respectfully and cleanly, without tasting beforehand, unnecessary kitchen talk or contamination between offered and unoffered food.
