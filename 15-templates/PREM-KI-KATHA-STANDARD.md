# Prem-kī-Kathā Standard

**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Effective:** 2026-06-30

## Definition

A valid **Prem-kī-Kathā** is an actual source-grounded devotional narrative — not a modern problem statement, not a one-paragraph hook, and not an invented drama attributed to sacred personalities.

## Valid source families

- Śrīla Prabhupāda's books (paraphrase + link; no full purports in Git)
- Kṛṣṇa-līlā and Caitanya-līlā from recognized Gauḍīya sources
- Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam narratives
- Recognized Gauḍīya history
- Verified Śrīla Prabhupāda-līlā (when documented)

## Required qualities

- Connected naturally to the module principle
- Devotional and heart-engaging
- Understandable to adults and children together
- Free of invented quotations attributed to sacred personalities
- Free of unsupported dramatic details
- Clear labeling: **source narrative**, **paraphrase**, or **facilitator transition**

## Length

- **12–15 minutes** narrated (normally 900–1,500 words depending on interaction)
- Plus **3–5 minutes** simple family interaction

## Required structure (in `prem-ki-katha.md`)

1. Katha title
2. Module connection
3. Primary source references and links
4. Setting and devotional mood
5. Main personalities
6. Source-grounded narrative
7. Turning point
8. Central teaching
9. Heart reflection
10. Two Lāla–Lālī interaction cues
11. Two Kiśora–Kiśorī reflection cues
12. Parent bridge
13. Transition to the philosophy lesson
14. Narration cautions
15. Visual/storyboard plan
16. Rights and quotation status
17. Human doctrinal review status

## What is not Prem-kī-Kathā

- One-paragraph contemporary hooks
- Gamma card text only
- Unsourced "inspirational" stories
- Combined overload of three unrelated līlās in one session
