# 1. Executive Design

KUTUMBA should be ambitious in sacred sound, family participation and practical devotional service, but conservative in ritual authority. The program succeeds when every family can participate meaningfully, understand what it sings, establish a realistic home practice and serve safely. It fails when ritual roles are rotated before competence, songs proliferate without control, or musical performance displaces hearing the holy name.

| **BOUNDARY** | **Recommended launch decision
**Launch with congregational kīrtana, approved prayers, prasāda prayers, japa education, simple instrument practice, altar-cleanliness education and low-risk sevā laboratories. Do not launch rotating ārati, formal group bhoga offerings, Deity handling or independent Tulasī leaf collection until written procedures and named servants are approved. |
| --- | --- |

## 1.1 Design Outcomes

| **Outcome domain** | **Year-One result** | **Evidence** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Knowledge | Families know the purpose and basic meaning of the core prayers, holy name, guru principle, prasāda, Tulasī and home worship. | Short explanation, learner sheet review and scenario questions |
| Skill | Participants can respond in kīrtana, use instruments appropriately, chant japa, maintain a sacred space and complete selected sevā. | Observation checklist and supervised demonstration |
| Practice | Each family maintains one realistic chanting, reading, offering or worship routine. | Private family practice review |
| Character | Service is marked by humility, punctuality, cleanliness, cooperation and receptivity to correction. | Trainer observation over time |
| Safety | No preventable sound, flame, trip, child-supervision or hygiene incident. | Pre-session checks and incident log |
| Authority | Restricted practices are performed only by named, authorised servants. | Current authorisation register |

## 1.2 Core Principles

- The Hare Kṛṣṇa mahā-mantra remains primary in KUTUMBA kīrtana; recognised prayers and bhajanas remain distinct items. [R2]

- The congregation is not divided into male/female, adult/child or similar response teams. [R2]

- Instrument choices follow the context: ārati kīrtana is not treated as a general stage performance. [R3]

- Hearing and chanting remain the life of worship; technique and ritual do not become substitutes for śravaṇam and kīrtanam. [R10]

- No family is pushed into a home-worship standard it cannot maintain consistently.

- Growth is private and non-competitive. No public comparison of rounds, song memory, instrument skill or service visibility.

- Children participate in service but do not carry ritual, flame or supervision responsibility.

# 2. Authority, Governance and Decision Rights

The system needs one clear line of authority. Informal “this is how we do it at home” practices are not automatically suitable for a shared ISKCON-aligned program. The GBC has historically required authorised research and approval for Deity-worship standards, while the ISKCON Deity Worship Ministry maintains structured training and procedures. [R4][R16]

## 2.1 Practice Classes

| **Class** | **Scope** | **Who may teach/perform** | **Approval** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **A — Congregational** | Mahā-mantra, common praṇāmas, approved prayers, response singing, basic japa, simple instrument exercises. | Approved KUTUMBA trainer or leader. | Canon and audio must be approved. |
| **B — Guided household** | Simple sacred space, food-offering education, Tulasī care, tilaka, kanthi, Ekādaśī, cleaning and garlands. | Approved trainer; family applies within defined scope. | Written household standard and local guidance. |
| **C — Restricted worship** | Formal ārati, formal group bhoga offering, Deity handling, abhiṣeka, restricted mantras, training others in formal worship. | Named, individually authorised servant. | Qualified ISKCON authority approval; service-specific authorisation. |

## 2.2 Roles and Decision Rights

| **Role** | **Accountability** | **May decide** | **May not decide alone** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Qualified ISKCON Authority | Confirms theology and exact worship standards. | Approve, reject or amend restricted procedures and trainer qualifications. | KUTUMBA attendance, family-care or operational policy. |
| Program Director | Owns integration with charter and temple relationship. | Adopt approved framework, appoint leads, pause the program. | Change an exact worship standard contrary to authority review. |
| Kīrtana & Worship Lead | Owns canon, training, equipment and service register. | Schedule approved content; approve Class A leaders within policy. | Authorise Class C service without required review. |
| Children & Safety Lead | Owns child, sound, flame, venue and incident controls. | Stop any activity immediately for safety. | Change theological standards. |
| Trainer | Demonstrates, observes and records competence. | Recommend progression or remediation. | Self-authorise or approve a spouse/family member without independent review. |
| Family Mentor | Supports newcomers and home practice. | Coach low-risk participation. | Perform confidential surveillance or pressure commitments. |

## 2.3 Approval Lifecycle

1. Draft the learner-facing or procedure document with a source register and explicit unresolved questions.

1. Internal review by the Kīrtana and Worship Lead, Curriculum Lead and Safety Lead.

1. Qualified ISKCON authority review for all exact worship procedures and disputed canon items.

1. Pilot as demonstration or dry practice only, where safe and permitted.

1. Record approval date, scope, trainer, expiry/review date and version.

1. Publish only the approved copy; archive and mark all prior copies superseded.

1. Review after an incident, doctrinal correction, venue change or material change in local temple guidance.

## 2.4 Change-Control Rules

- A new song request requires author/source, exact text, translation source, intended occasion, audio reference and reviewer decision.

- A preferred melody is not a new canonical text. Melody versions are still controlled because pronunciation and mood can change.

- No one edits mantras on a projected slide minutes before the program.

- No “temporary” ritual step becomes permanent through repetition. Temporary deviations are recorded and reviewed.

- Emergency safety changes can stop or simplify a practice immediately; they cannot create a new ritual standard.

## 4.2 Explicit Exclusions

- Movie melodies, commercial devotional remixes or tunes whose main association is non-devotional entertainment.

- Newly invented mantras, slogans or repetitive formulations not introduced by Śrīla Prabhupāda, previous authorised ācāryas or śāstra. [R2]

- Songs with uncertain authorship or meaning that the leader cannot explain.

- Confidential or advanced līlā songs unsuitable for a mixed beginner family program.

- Any text copied from a random video description without verification against a controlled source.

- Unapproved changes to names, word endings, order or translation for musical convenience.

# 5. Meaning, Pronunciation and Memorisation

## 5.1 Required Learner-Sheet Fields

| **Field** | **Required content** |
| --- | --- |
| Identity | Canon ID, title, author/ācārya, textual source and approved version. |
| Text | IAST transliteration; optional Devanāgarī/Bengali only when useful. |
| Learner line | Syllable-separated pronunciation aid without replacing the canonical text. |
| Audio | One approved lead-and-response file and one slow pronunciation file. |
| Meaning | Brief overall meaning plus key words; no unverified translation. |
| Occasion | When it is sung, and when it is not. |
| Learning level | Listen, respond, recite, memorise, lead or explain. |
| Control | Reviewer, approval date, version and next review date. |

## 5.2 Pronunciation Key

| **Symbol** | **Practical guide** | **Common error to avoid** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ā | long a as in father | Do not shorten names such as Rādhā. |
| ī | long ee | Do not pronounce as short i. |
| ū | long oo | Keep the vowel clean, not a diphthong. |
| ṛ | syllabic r, taught through approved audio | Avoid forcing an English “ree” everywhere. |
| c | ch as in church | Not k or s. |
| ś / ṣ | sh sounds with distinct articulation | Beginners may approximate, but leaders should train accurately. |
| ṅ / ñ / ṇ | nasal consonants determined by position | Do not delete the nasal sound. |
| ai | approximately eye | Avoid flat English “ay.” |
| e / o | normally long, clean vowels | Avoid reducing to a weak schwa. |

# 6. Friday Kīrtana Operating Standard

## 6.1 Launch-Phase Sequence

| **Time** | **Element** | **Operating standard** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 6:30–6:33 | Settle | Phones silent; instruments ready; one short etiquette reminder; no social talk in the worship area. |
| 6:33–6:37 | Praṇāma and Pañca-tattva | Approved text, moderate pace, no improvised additions. |
| 6:37–6:52 | Mahā-mantra kīrtana | Primary mantra; repeatable phrases; congregation clearly audible. |
| 6:52–6:58 | Cycle prayer or bhajana | One current learning item with brief context, not a medley. |
| 6:58–7:00 | Close and transition | Deliberate ending; no abrupt microphone drop; instruments secured. |

## 6.2 Congregation-First Rules

- The leader sings one complete, repeatable phrase and allows the full response before continuing.

- The melody remains in a range that ordinary adults and children can sing.

- Tempo changes are gradual; speed is not used to manufacture excitement.

- The lead microphone remains clear but not dominant; the response must be heard.

- No vocal contest, prolonged ornamentation, solo performance or instrumental break without chanting.

- No division of response by gender, age or other bodily category. [R2]

- Children may lead a prepared short portion beside an approved adult; they are not placed on display unexpectedly.

- The kīrtana leader watches the congregation, safety conditions and program clock—not only the musicians.

## 6.3 Song Selection Decision Tree

1. Is the text present in the approved canon register? If no, do not use it.

1. Is it appropriate to the occasion and current curriculum? If no, choose the assigned item.

1. Does the leader know the exact text, meaning and approved melody? If no, lead the mahā-mantra instead.

1. Does the instrument set match the context, especially if ārati is occurring? If no, remove the instrument.

1. Can the congregation respond comfortably? If no, simplify pitch, phrase length or tempo.

1. Is sound safe and clear? If no, reduce amplification immediately.

# 7. Kīrtana-Leader and Response-Singing Training

## 7.1 Four-Level Pathway

| **Level** | **Minimum capability** | **Evidence** | **Privilege** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| L1 Participant | Follows response; knows Tier 0 texts; observes etiquette. | Four satisfactory sessions. | Congregational participation. |
| L2 Assistant | Starts response; supports newcomers; manages sheets/instruments; basic kartāla. | Four assisted sessions and trainer feedback. | Response support and setup. |
| L3 Supervised leader | Leads Pañca-tattva, 8–12 minutes of mahā-mantra and one approved close. | Two satisfactory observed leads. | Lead only with trainer present. |
| L4 Approved leader | Consistent accuracy, congregation awareness, time control, humility and safety. | Written approval and review date. | Lead assigned KUTUMBA openings. |
| L5 Trainer | Can diagnose pronunciation, tempo, sound, instrument and interpersonal issues. | Authority/lead appointment plus demonstrated coaching. | Train and recommend leaders; not self-authorise restricted worship. |

## 7.2 Observation Rubric

| **Criterion** | **Meets standard when…** | **Red flag** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Text | Words are exact and intelligible. | Improvised or repeatedly incorrect mantra. |
| Pitch | Range is singable for the room. | Leader sings for personal vocal display. |
| Phrase | Response can copy it in one hearing. | Long, changing phrases that exclude newcomers. |
| Tempo | Steady and proportionate to the setting. | Abrupt acceleration, dragging or competition. |
| Volume | Clear, sweet and not painful. | Distortion, shouting or instruments cover mantra. |
| Awareness | Leader sees children, newcomers and transitions. | Eyes closed throughout while conditions deteriorate. |
| Conduct | Accepts correction and shares service. | Defensiveness, status-seeking or repeated policy violations. |
| Ending | Closes together and hands over calmly. | Sudden stop, confusion or overrun. |

## 7.3 Response-Singing Clinic

- Listen to the full phrase before responding.

- Begin together; do not anticipate the leader.

- Copy words first, melody second and ornamentation last.

- Stay with the leader’s basic melody; do not create competing harmonies.

- Use a devotional, sustainable voice rather than a forced shout.

- Make space for newcomers to listen without embarrassment.

- Reduce volume and complexity when young children or sensory-sensitive participants are present.

## 7.4 Remediation and Withdrawal

Approval is service-specific and revocable. A leader moves back to supervised practice after repeated text errors, unapproved songs, unsafe volume, refusal of correction, disregard for the congregation, poor reliability or behaviour inconsistent with the family covenant. Withdrawal is handled privately and framed as protection of service—not punishment.

# 8. Kartāla and Mṛdaṅga Training

| **APPROVED PRACTICE** | **Instrument standard

## 8.1 Kartāla Pathway

| **Stage** | **Content** | **Pass condition** |

## 8.2 Mṛdaṅga Pathway

| **Stage** | **Content** | **Boundary** |
| --- | --- | --- |

## 8.3 Instrument-Care Standard

- Number and label instruments; record condition before and after each cycle.

- Store dry, stable and away from food, heaters, direct sun and child play areas.

- Do not stack weight on mṛdaṅgas or leave kartālas loose where they become trip hazards.

- Report cracks, loose straps, sharp edges or tuning problems; do not improvise repairs during the program.

- Only the trainer tunes or performs material repairs unless a separate qualified person is named.

# 9. Japa Formation

KUTUMBA teaches japa as attentive personal chanting, not as a public score. Śrī Caitanya’s Śikṣāṣṭakam connects chanting with humility and respect, and Śrīla Prabhupāda repeatedly emphasises chanting on beads under guidance. [R8][R9]

## 9.1 Four Modules

| **Module** | **Learning outcomes** | **Laboratory** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| J1 — What japa is | Purpose of the mahā-mantra; hearing one’s own chanting; respectful bead care. | Five-minute guided chant and reflection. |
| J2 — Mechanics | Posture, bag/bead handling, pronunciation, sustainable pace and session start/end. | Trainer demonstration; individual practice. |
| J3 — Attention | Notice distraction, return to sound, manage phone/environment, avoid mechanical speed. | Two short rounds with a reset between them. |
| J4 — Family practice | Adults model without policing; children receive age-appropriate invitations; commitments remain realistic. | Family routine design and private review. |

## 9.2 Commitment Rules

- No public round-count reporting, ranking or reward.

- A new participant may begin with a time-based or small-count commitment and increase only when stable.

- No child is shamed, physically restrained or repeatedly corrected during japa.

- Families may use a quiet corner, walking japa or shorter focused periods where appropriate.

- Leaders distinguish inspiration from initiation standards; KUTUMBA does not promise initiation qualification.

- Persistent attention, mental-health or sensory concerns are handled compassionately and are not treated as moral failure.

## 9.3 Private Review Questions

- When and where is chanting most likely to happen consistently?

- Can I hear each word clearly?

- What is the main predictable distraction, and what one control will I try?

- Is my commitment honest and sustainable for the next cycle?

- What support do I need from my family without turning them into monitors?

# 11. Home Sacred Space and Household Worship

The default Year-One goal is a clean, intentional sacred space—normally beginning with approved pictures and a practice the family can maintain. Formal Deity ownership is not a Year-One success metric. ISKCON’s own home-worship training distinguishes home and temple worship, covers purity, food offering, Tulasī, ārati, children, travel and offences, and builds each topic progressively. [R4]

## 11.1 Minimum Viable Sacred Space

- A stable, clean location above ordinary floor clutter and away from shoes, laundry, television and food storage.

- Only approved pictures and devotional items; no crowded mixture of unrelated religious or decorative objects.

- Clean cloth, frames and surface; dedicated cleaning materials where practical.

- A realistic daily or weekly attention routine—such as prayer, family chanting, reading or a simple approved offering.

- A travel and illness plan so the practice does not become a source of guilt or neglect.

- No formal Deity acquisition without individual guidance about standard, continuity and family capacity.

## 11.2 Home-Space Review

| **Domain** | **Green** | **Amber** | **Red** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Location | Clean, stable and respectful. | Shared space but controllable. | Near shoes, clutter, unsafe heat or frequent disturbance. |
| Content | Approved and understandable. | Some items need review. | Unapproved Deities, ritual objects or mixed procedure. |
| Routine | Small and consistent. | Inconsistent but realistic to correct. | Elaborate promise with no capacity. |
| Food offering | Approved household method. | Learning/shadow stage. | Improvised mantras or unclear offered/unoffered separation. |
| Children | Included safely and positively. | Needs clearer roles. | Children carry flame, knives, heavy items or responsibility. |
| Cleanliness | Simple supplies and routine. | Occasional lapses. | Persistent contamination, pests or neglect. |

## 11.3 Family Workshop Deliverable

Each family leaves the home-altar workshop with one drawing or photograph-free plan, one cleaning routine, one agreed prayer/chanting practice, one food-offering next step and one question for the approved trainer. No family is asked to display its home publicly or invite inspection without consent.

# 16. Worship Safety and Cleanliness

## 16.1 Sound Safety

GBC guidance calls for clear, sweet sound rather than painful loudness and encourages sound-level monitoring. NIOSH treats 85 dBA over eight hours as a hazardous occupational threshold; CDC guidance for children emphasises reducing loud music, limiting exposure and increasing distance. [R2][R11][R12]

| **Control** | **KUTUMBA rule** |
| --- | --- |
| Target | Aim for approximately 75–80 dBA in the children’s area during amplified kīrtana. |
| Reduction point | Reduce immediately if sound is sustained near/above 85 dBA, distorted, painful, or ordinary speech at arm’s length requires shouting. |
| Placement | Never point a speaker directly at seated children or place them close to a monitor. |
| Measurement | Use one designated meter/device at the children’s zone; record only exceptions/incidents, not every session. |
| Sensitive participants | Offer distance, quiet seating or hearing protection without stigma. |
| Authority | Safety Lead can lower or shut off amplification without debate. |

## 16.2 Open-Flame Safety

NFPA religious-candle guidance stresses sturdy holders, continuous adult supervision near altars, keeping flames away from curtains and storing matches/lighters away from children. KUTUMBA adds stricter child and movement controls because ārati involves handheld flame. [R13]

- Obtain written or clearly documented venue permission and comply with local fire code and alarm/sprinkler rules.

- Only an authorised adult handles a lit ārati lamp; no passing a lit lamp from person to person.

- Maintain a clear flame zone with stable footing, secured hair/clothing and no dancing or children inside the zone.

- Place hot articles on a stable non-combustible tray at a named cooling location.

- Keep an appropriate extinguisher and smothering method accessible; a trained adult knows how to use them.

- Store matches and lighters locked or inaccessible to children.

- Any dropped lamp, burn, flare-up or unsafe crowd movement stops the service and triggers an incident review.

- Where flame is prohibited, do not improvise a ritual substitute. Use an approved non-flame program design or temple-based practical training.

## 16.3 Cleanliness Standard

| **Area** | **Minimum standard** | **Stop condition** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Person | Clean hands/clothing; wounds covered; hair controlled for food/flame service. | Illness affecting food safety, uncovered wound or contamination. |
| Worship area | Shoes, bags, coats, food containers and clutter outside defined sacred area. | Trip hazard, pest evidence or unclean surface. |
| Articles | Labelled, intact, clean and separately stored. | Damage, unknown residue or missing required article. |
| Food interface | Offered/unoffered separation; dedicated plate/utensils; no tasting. | Unclear status, allergen confusion or contamination. |
| Cloths | Altar, instrument, food and floor cloths separated. | Cross-use or unknown cloth status. |
| Hands | Wash after cleaning, plant/flower work and before food/worship handling. | No accessible handwashing or approved alternative. |

## 16.4 Risk Register

| **Risk** | **Impact** | **Primary controls** | **Owner** | **Stop trigger** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Excessive sound | Hearing discomfort/injury | Meter, speaker placement, conservative target | Safety Lead | Pain, distortion, >85 dBA sustained |
| Open flame | Burn/fire | Authorised adult, clear zone, venue permission | Safety Lead | Dropped lamp, child entry, unstable surface |
| Hot paraphernalia | Burn | Cooling station and adult-only handling | Worship Lead | No safe placement |
| Crowded dancing | Collision/fall | Defined movement area and child supervision | Welcome/Safety | Running, blocked exits |
| Instrument misuse | Injury/damage | Controlled distribution and storage | Instrument Trainer | Swinging, sharp/damaged item |
| Tools in sevā lab | Cut/puncture | Age controls and direct supervision | Lab Trainer | Unsupervised needle/scissors |
| Allergy/plant exposure | Reaction | Forms, labels, gloves/alternatives where needed | Safety/Operations | Symptoms or unknown material |
| Unapproved procedure | Spiritual/operational error | Controlled documents and stop authority | Worship Lead | Procedure or text cannot be verified |
